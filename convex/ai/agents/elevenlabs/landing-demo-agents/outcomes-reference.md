# Shared Outcomes Reference

## Purpose

These are the concrete benchmark outcomes the landing-page demo agents should sound anchored to.

They exist because the landing page already promises specific results by agent. The demo should feel operational and measurable, not abstract.

## Truthfulness rule

These are reference outcomes for the fictional demo business. They should be described as:

- modeled outcomes in the demo environment
- realistic benchmarks based on the kind of deployments sevenlayers targets

They should not be described as audited public case-study claims unless the team has a real customer source for that exact number.

## Clara: Virtual Receptionist

### Baseline

- business-hours missed-call rate: `17%`
- overflow / after-hours missed-call leakage: `38%`
- callback delay: `2.6 hours`

### Reference outcome

- effective answer coverage: `24/7`
- missed-call leakage reduced to under `4%`
- lead response under `10 minutes`

### Safe phrasing

- "In this demo business, the front desk stopped leaking calls across both locations."
- "The point is not just answering faster. The point is answering with enough context to book and route immediately."

## Maren: Appointment Coordination

### Baseline

- no-show rate: `11%`
- cancellation recovery is manual and inconsistent
- callers often pick the wrong location because they do not know where capacity exists

### Reference outcome

- no-show rate reduced to `7%`
- over `80%` of cancellations are rebooked or backfilled
- cross-location booking prevents wasted capacity

### Safe phrasing

- "The demo shows how one scheduling layer can fill the next useful slot instead of leaving capacity idle."
- "The modeled result here is fewer no-shows and faster recovery when cancellations happen."

## Jonas: Lead Qualification

### Baseline

- inbound leads are mixed together
- the sales team spends time talking to low-intent inquiries
- qualification notes are inconsistent

### Reference outcome

- qualified route decision in under `60 seconds`
- hot leads moved quickly to the right owner
- every lead ends with a structured summary instead of free-text notes

### Safe phrasing

- "The value is not just speed. It is that the next person sees a clean summary instead of re-asking everything."

## Tobias: Field Documentation

### Baseline

- same-day quote send rate: `28%`
- rough voice notes turn into admin backlog
- field teams delay documentation until evening

### Reference outcome

- same-day quote send rate rises to `72%`
- job notes become structured draft inputs immediately
- fewer quotes slip because details were lost in the handoff

### Safe phrasing

- "This demo is built around reducing the gap between what happened on site and what gets sent to the customer."

## Lina: Customer Follow-Up

### Baseline

- open quotes age without follow-up
- post-visit follow-up is inconsistent
- happy customers are rarely asked for reviews at the right moment

### Reference outcome

- unworked open quotes after 5 days drop from `34%` to `12%`
- high-risk customers are flagged earlier
- review volume grows because requests are timed properly

### Safe phrasing

- "The result is not more messages. It is better-timed messages with a reason behind them."

## Kai: Team Operations

### Baseline

- managers spend `5.5 hours/week` on staffing coordination
- coverage requests are fragmented across calls and chats
- handoffs are inconsistent

### Reference outcome

- coordination time drops to `1.5 hours/week`
- coverage gaps are identified and routed faster
- handoffs are more complete and easier to track

### Safe phrasing

- "The operational win here is less chasing and fewer dropped handoffs."

## Nora: Location Intelligence

### Baseline

- KPI reporting lag: `3-5 days`
- underperforming locations are found too late
- managers act on instinct instead of shared numbers

### Reference outcome

- same-day view of answer rate, booking rate, no-show rate, and customer satisfaction
- weak locations stand out immediately
- managers get weekly insight instead of raw spreadsheet noise

### Safe phrasing

- "The main shift is from delayed reporting to active operations management."

## Demo guidance

1. Keep numbers consistent across agents.
2. Use outcomes as proof of operational change, not hype.
3. If a caller asks for the source of a number, explain that this is a fictional demo company modeled on the patterns seen in real operator deployments.
