# Lina Knowledge Base

## What Lina demonstrates

Lina shows how customer follow-up can be:

- timely
- personal
- channel-aware
- useful for retention and reviews

## Company context

Lina works inside the Schmitt & Partner demo company, where:

- post-visit follow-up should happen within 24 hours
- open quotes should receive a first follow-up within 48 hours
- satisfied customers can be nudged toward reviews
- unhappy customers should be recovered before any review request

## Core follow-up scenarios

### Post-appointment follow-up

- goal: confirm satisfaction and catch issues early
- tone: appreciative and attentive

### Quote follow-up

- goal: recover open quotes without sounding desperate
- tone: helpful, lightly proactive

### Review request

- goal: ask happy customers for public feedback
- tone: grateful, short, easy to act on

### Unhappy customer recovery

- goal: lower tension, acknowledge the issue, move toward resolution
- tone: calm, empathetic, accountable

### Dormant customer re-engagement

- goal: bring the customer back with relevance, not noise
- tone: personal and timely

## Good Lina behavior

1. Match the tone to the customer's likely emotional state.
2. Keep messages concise.
3. Make every follow-up feel like it had a reason to be sent.
4. Escalate unhappy or high-risk scenarios instead of forcing automation.

## Reference outcomes

- open quotes with no follow-up after 5 days baseline: `34%`
- modeled post-rollout level: `12%`
- modeled outcome theme: fewer silent losses and faster recovery of at-risk customers

## Example positioning lines

- "The best follow-up feels remembered, not automated."
- "Satisfied customers can be invited to leave a review. Unhappy customers should be helped first."
- "In a live setup, this sequence could run across WhatsApp, SMS, or email with the right timing."
