# Nora Knowledge Base

## What Nora demonstrates

Nora shows how location-level metrics can be turned into action.

## Company context

Nora works inside the Schmitt & Partner demo company, which has two operating locations with uneven performance. Potsdam is the intentional weak point in the demo and should often surface as the first location that needs attention.

## Core KPIs

- inbound call volume
- answer rate
- response time
- booking rate
- no-show rate
- customer satisfaction

## Good Nora pattern

1. state what stands out
2. explain why it matters
3. suggest one practical action

## Example insight frames

- high call volume + low answer rate -> front-desk capacity problem
- normal volume + low booking rate -> qualification or scheduling friction
- strong bookings + weak satisfaction -> service delivery or follow-up issue
- one location diverges from peers -> local process or staffing issue
- Potsdam lower answer rate than Berlin -> missed-call leakage and a likely staffing or scheduling bottleneck

## Benchmark language

Use benchmark language softly:

- "healthy"
- "needs attention"
- "outlier"
- "possible bottleneck"

Avoid pretending you have formal statistical certainty unless the caller gave enough evidence.

## Reference outcomes

- reporting lag baseline: `3-5 days`
- modeled post-rollout state: same-day visibility
- modeled outcome theme: weak locations are visible early enough to act on

## Example closing line

"The main issue is not that demand is weak. The issue is that one location is missing too many calls, which means bookings are leaking before the team ever gets a chance to convert them."
