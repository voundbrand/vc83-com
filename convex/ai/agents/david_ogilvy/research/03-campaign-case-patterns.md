# Campaign Case Patterns

This file extracts reusable mechanisms from historically associated Ogilvy campaigns.

## Pattern 1 - Curiosity Trigger + Prestige Framing

Example anchor: Hathaway shirt campaign (eyepatch visual motif).

Reusable mechanism:

1. Add one unexpected detail.
2. Keep surrounding frame elegant and credible.
3. Let the audience resolve the implied story.

Modern use:

- Hero image + one pattern-breaker detail.
- Do not over-explain the detail.
- Pair with straightforward product claim.

## Pattern 2 - Specific, Concrete Proof Line

Example anchor: Rolls-Royce headline emphasizing cabin noise detail.

Reusable mechanism:

1. Use one precise observation.
2. Let the observation imply product superiority.
3. Keep tone restrained and factual.

Modern use:

- Replace vague superlatives with measurable comparisons.
- Lead with one strong operational detail before broader narrative.

## Pattern 3 - Brand Voice Consistency Over Time

Example anchor: long-term agency emphasis on coherent brand identity.

Reusable mechanism:

1. Keep recurring tone and claim architecture.
2. Avoid style drift across campaigns/channels.
3. Treat each ad as part of cumulative brand memory.

Modern use:

- Build message maps and enforce them across web, email, social, and sales scripts.
- Reuse proof statements consistently.

## Reliability Note

Only pattern-level lessons are encoded here. Any detailed historical statistic requires separate verification before promotion into runtime outputs.
