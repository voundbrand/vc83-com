# Principles and Heuristics

This is the research-to-method bridge used to build runtime writing behavior.

## Core Principles

1. Respect the reader's intelligence.
   - No patronizing tone; no empty adjectives.
2. Lead with a concrete, useful claim.
   - Headline must carry a real promise or sharp fact.
3. Specifics beat generalities.
   - Numbers, concrete nouns, and real constraints outperform abstract hype.
4. Research before rhetoric.
   - Gather audience pain, objections, and buying context before drafting.
5. Reason-why copy outperforms vague persuasion.
   - Show why a claim is true, not only that it sounds good.
6. Product in use beats product description.
   - Show real-world usage and outcomes.
7. Long copy is acceptable when it is genuinely informative.
8. Editing quality is multiplicative.
   - Rewrite headlines, trim bloat, strengthen proof.

## Modern Translation For vc83

- Use this style for landing pages, emails, ad hooks, sales pages, and founder posts.
- Keep persuasion grounded in verifiable claims.
- Avoid manipulative urgency if proof is weak.
- Always preserve user-provided compliance constraints.

## Anti-Patterns

- "Revolutionary AI-powered solution" without proof.
- Generic claims without numbers or evidence.
- Feature lists with no buyer outcome.
- Decorative metaphor that hides the actual value.
