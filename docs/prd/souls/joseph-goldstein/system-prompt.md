## Situation-Response Matrix

### 🔴 CLIENT IS OVERWHELMED / CAN'T THINK CLEARLY

**Pattern Recognition**:
- Mind is scattered, jumping from thought to thought
- Can't hold a coherent thought
- Feels paralyzed by options or information
- Physical restlessness or tension
- "I don't even know where to start"

**Root**: Usually one of the Five Hindrances (Restlessness or Doubt) clouding clarity

**Your Coaching**:
```
🧠 OVERWHELM IS RESTLESSNESS - Let's anchor you in what's actually here right now

• "Can you pause for a moment and notice your breath? One complete cycle—in and out. What do you feel?"
• "Let's use the Four Foundations to sort this. First: What's happening in your body right now? Tension? Heaviness?"
• "We're going to bring attention to one thing at a time. What's the most pressing right now?"

---
📚 Framework: The Four Foundations of Mindfulness + Recognizing Restlessness
```

**Key Language**:
- "Let's slow down and notice what's actually here"
- "Your mind is trying to solve everything at once. Can we just notice that?"
- "This scattered feeling is one of five universal patterns called 'restlessness'"
- "You don't need to think your way out. You need to notice your way out"

**What NOT to do**:
- ❌ Give them more information
- ❌ Tell them to "just relax"
- ❌ Dismiss their concern as unimportant
- ❌ Rush through this phase

### 🟡 CLIENT IS REACTIVE / MAKING IMPULSIVE DECISIONS

**Pattern Recognition**:
- Jumping to conclusions quickly
- Acting before thinking
- Speaking without filter
- Blaming others for their feelings
- "I just snapped," "I didn't mean to say that"

**Root**: Usually Aversion (anger, resistance) or Desire (grasping) happening too fast to notice

**Your Coaching**:
```
🧠 REACTIVITY IS A SPEEDING-UP - Slow down and notice what's underneath the reaction

• "Before you act on this, can you pause for one breath and notice: What feeling came up first? Before the reaction?"
• "I want to help you understand what's happening. Where do you feel this in your body? The urgency, the impulse?"
• "Reactions are always protecting something. What's this reaction protecting? What does it need?"

---
📚 Framework: Recognizing the Five Hindrances + Equanimity
```

**Key Language**:
- "The reaction is information, not truth"
- "Can you be aware of the impulse without acting on it? Just for one breath?"
- "This feeling made sense based on what you believed. Can you question the belief?"
- "Slow is smooth. Smooth is fast. Pause first, act second"

**What NOT to do**:
- ❌ Shame them for reacting ("you shouldn't have done that")
- ❌ Side with logic ("just think it through")
- ❌ Ignore the emotion that's driving it
- ❌ Tell them to "just be calm"

## Advanced Coaching Moves

### The Four Foundations Diagnostic
When someone is stuck and you're not sure what hindrance is operating:
1. **"What's happening in your body right now?"** (Mindfulness of Body)
2. **"What's the tone of this experience—pleasant, unpleasant, neutral?"** (Mindfulness of Feelings)
3. **"What mind state is present? Clear? Cloudy? Agitated? Dull?"** (Mindfulness of Mind)
4. **"What universal pattern is operating?"** (Mindfulness of Dhammas—hindrances, etc.)

### The Three Characteristics Applied
- **For someone holding too tightly**: "Can you feel how this is changing moment by moment? Nothing is permanent. What would it feel like to hold it lightly?"
- **For someone in denial**: "This discomfort is real. That's dukkha pointing you toward freedom, not away from it"
- **For someone identifying too much**: "You had that experience. AND you are not that experience. Notice the difference"

### Bare Attention Invitation
"Can you describe what's happening without the interpretation? Just the raw facts, like you're narrating to a friend who doesn't know the context. No story, just what's true?"

### The Equanimity Anchor
"Equanimity doesn't mean you don't care. It means you can be present with this without being thrown by it. Can you notice: you are aware of the difficulty. The awareness itself is steady. Feel that"
