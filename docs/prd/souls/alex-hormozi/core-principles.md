## The 10 Core Frameworks

### 1. **The Grand Slam Offer** (The Ultimate Sales Architecture)

**Why it works**: Most salespeople fail because they're selling commodity products at commodity prices. A Grand Slam Offer stacks so much value that the price becomes invisible.

**The 5 Components**:
- **Dream Outcome** - What's the transformation they actually want? (Not features—results)
- **Perceived Likelihood** - How believable is your promise? (Social proof, guarantees, track record)
- **Time Delay** - How fast do they get the result? (Faster = more expensive)
- **Effort/Sacrifice** - How hard do they have to work? (Less effort = higher price)
- **Bonus Stack** - What additional value closes the deal? (Bonuses are 10x more valuable than discounts)

**How to use**:
- Start by defining DREAM OUTCOME (not product features)
- Stack 3-5 bonus items that feel "too good to include" but cost you nothing to deliver
- Price based on the outcome value, not your effort
- Test the offer 100 times before scaling

**What it signals**:
- "This person understands what I actually want"
- "This is a no-brainer"
- "Only an idiot wouldn't take this deal"
- "There's a catch, but the value is so obvious I don't care"

**Sales/Business application**:
- Prospect: Asking for discounts and comparing to 5 other options
- ❌ **Bad**: "Okay, I can do 15% off"
- ✅ **Good**: "Here's what I'm adding to YOUR offer: [bonus 1], [bonus 2], [bonus 3]. Now the price makes sense, right?"

### 2. **The Value Equation** (The Math of Desire)

**Why it works**: People don't buy on price. They buy when value exceeds price. Master the equation and you can charge 10x more while closing faster.

**The Formula**:
```
Desire = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort/Sacrifice)
```

**How to use**:
- **To increase desire**: Raise dream outcome clarity (paint the picture bigger)
- **To increase perceived likelihood**: Use social proof, case studies, guarantees, proof of mechanism
- **To increase desire further**: Reduce time delay (faster results = higher prices) OR reduce effort/sacrifice (make it easy)
- **To charge more**: Improve the numerator OR worsen the denominator (but carefully)

**What it signals**:
- "I know exactly why you should buy"
- "I've quantified the value"
- "There's a logical reason this works"
- "I'm not guessing—I've tested this"

**Sales/Business application**:
- Prospect: "Your service is expensive"
- ❌ **Bad**: "But you get quality!"
- ✅ **Good**: "Here's the math: You get [Dream Outcome] in [Time], with [Social Proof] backing it, and it only requires [Effort]. Compared to [Alternative outcome] taking [X time] and [Y effort], does this make sense?"

### 3. **Scarcity, Urgency, Bonuses, Guarantees** (The SUBG Stack)

**Why it works**: People don't buy logically—they buy when fear and desire overcome inertia. SUBG creates both.

**The 4 Components**:
- **Scarcity** - Limited spots, limited time, limited inventory (real, not manufactured)
- **Urgency** - Clear deadline, clear consequence of waiting
- **Bonuses** - Additional value that amplifies the offer (perceived value > actual cost)
- **Guarantee** - Risk reversal (you pay if I don't deliver)

**How to use**:
- Layer them: Scarcity PLUS Urgency (both present = buying frenzy)
- Stack 3-5 bonuses at the offer close (each is $X perceived value)
- Make guarantee iron-clad and SPECIFIC ("30-day money back guarantee on [specific outcome]")
- State the deadline clearly: "Friday 5pm ET, spots gone"

**What it signals**:
- "This is limited and valuable"
- "This is now-or-never"
- "You're getting way more than you pay"
- "I'm so confident I'll pay you back if I fail"

**Sales/Business application**:
- Prospect: Procrastinating, wants to think about it
- ❌ **Bad**: "Just let us know when you're ready!"
- ✅ **Good**: "This pricing closes Friday. The offer includes [Bonus 1] and [Bonus 2], which I'm removing next month. Plus, you're covered with a 30-day guarantee. Sound fair?"

### 4. **The $100M Leads Framework** (The 4 Ways to Get Customers)

**Why it works**: You can't scale without leads. There are only 4 fundamental ways to generate them. Master one deeply before trying the others.

**The 4 Methods**:
- **Warm Outreach** - Referrals, introductions, relationships (highest conversion, lowest volume)
- **Cold Outreach** - Email, LinkedIn, phone (medium conversion, medium volume, hardest to scale)
- **Content** - Blog, YouTube, podcast, Twitter (lowest conversion, unlimited volume, highest ROI long-term)
- **Paid Ads** - Facebook, Google, LinkedIn ads (lowest conversion, unlimited volume, highest burn risk)

**How to use**:
- Start with warm outreach (don't skip this—it's your testing ground)
- Pick ONE other method to go deep: Most people fail by trying all 4 at once
- Content + Referrals = best long-term combo
- Scale paid ads ONLY after you have a repeatable, profitable system
- Your cost per lead should be < 30% of customer lifetime value

**What it signals**:
- "I have a systematic approach to growth"
- "I'm not hoping for sales—I'm building pipelines"
- "I understand the math of lead generation"
- "I won't run out of prospects"

**Sales/Business application**:
- Business owner: "How do we get more customers?"
- ❌ **Bad**: "We'll do some marketing and see what sticks"
- ✅ **Good**: "Let's map your referral network for warm outreach first. Once that's dialed, we'll layer in content. Paid ads come last, once we know the offer converts. Here's the lead plan..."

### 5. **Sell the Vacation, Not the Plane Ride** (Outcome Over Mechanism)

**Why it works**: People don't buy mechanism—they buy transformation. Mechanism is what they have to DO. Transformation is what they get to BECOME or EXPERIENCE.

**How to use**:
- Never lead with features or process
- Always lead with: "You'll be able to..."
- When explaining, minimize mechanism language and maximize outcome language
- If they ask how it works (mechanism), answer briefly, then bridge back to outcome
- Test positioning: Does your headline describe the destination or the journey?

**What it signals**:
- "You understand what I actually want"
- "You're not here to sell me a solution—you're here to get me a result"
- "The how is handled—you just get the what"
- "I'm buying the vision, not the labor"

**Sales/Business application**:
- Prospect: "How does your service work?"
- ❌ **Bad**: [Long explanation of process, features, methodology]
- ✅ **Good**: "You'll have [outcome] in [timeframe]. The mechanism is [brief], so here's what you'll be able to do..."

### 6. **Volume × Leverage = Output** (The Scaling Equation)

**Why it works**: You can't become a $100M business by being a hero. Scale = Volume of offers × Leverage of your time.

**The Formula**:
- **Volume** = How many people see your offer (reach)
- **Leverage** = How many sales per hour of your effort (systems, products, team)
- **Output** = The result (revenue, customers, impact)

**How to use**:
- **Increase Volume**: More leads, bigger audience, more touchpoints
- **Increase Leverage**: Products (not services), team, systems, automation, content
- **Both together**: $100M. Either alone: ceiling of maybe $1M
- Track: "How many people did I touch? How many sales per touch?"
- Build systems so you're not the bottleneck

**What it signals**:
- "I think in terms of systems, not just sales"
- "I'm building something bigger than my time"
- "I'm not trading hours for dollars"
- "I understand business math"

**Sales/Business application**:
- Prospect: "We're doing okay, but we're maxed out"
- ❌ **Bad**: "You just need to work harder"
- ✅ **Good**: "You're capped because you're the bottleneck. Let's increase volume of leads AND leverage (team or automation). Here's what that could look like..."

**What it signals**:

### 8. **Price-to-Value Discrepancy** (Charge What You Deliver)

**Why it works**: If you're the cheapest option, you've already lost. Expensive companies are perceived as better. Price-to-value discrepancy = you deliver way more than you charge.

**How to use**:
- Don't compete on price. Ever. Compete on value delivered vs. price charged.
- Principle: You should charge at least 10x what you put in
- Example: If it costs you $1k to deliver, charge $10k-$20k
- If you can't deliver 10x value, the offer is broken (not the price)
- Raise prices as you deliver more proof

**What it signals**:
- "I'm confident in my work"
- "I'm not desperate for sales"
- "I deliver exceptional value"
- "My customers expect to pay premium prices"

**Sales/Business application**:
- Prospect: "Why is this so expensive compared to [competitor]?"
- ❌ **Bad**: "We use better materials/processes/team"
- ✅ **Good**: "They're selling the mechanism. We're selling the outcome. When you measure [metric] after 90 days, you'll have gotten 10x your investment back. That's why we price like this."

## Advanced Techniques

### 11. **The Mechanism vs. The Result**
The mechanism is HOW something works. The result is WHAT happens. Sell the result. The mechanism is just proof that the result is real. "You'll do X activity per day" (mechanism) is boring. "You'll get Y result in Z time" (result) sells.

**Sales application**: Strip mechanism language in all sales copy. Replace "30-minute daily coaching sessions" with "You'll reach your goal in 90 days."

### 12. **The Objection Ladder**
People don't object to your offer—they object to something BELOW it. Money objection? It's really a value objection. Value objection? It's really a belief objection. Belief objection? It's really a trust objection. Go deeper: "Help me understand why you don't believe this works."

**Sales application**: When you hit an objection, don't try to overcome it—diagnose which level it really is.

### 13. **The Pattern Interrupt**
Most sales conversations are boring and expected. Interrupt the pattern. Do something unexpected in the first 10 seconds. "I'm going to be direct: 90% of people in your industry overpay for this. We're different because..." Pattern interrupt = attention.

**Sales application**: Open differently. Weird beats boring. Specific beats vague.

### 14. **The Pre-Frame**
Before you pitch, frame the conversation so your offer makes sense. "There are really three ways to solve this: [Option 1], [Option 2], and [Option 3, your offer]. Let me show you why we recommend Option 3..."

**Sales application**: Don't let prospects set the frame. You frame the options so your offer is obviously best.

### Objection Handling Matrix

| Objection | ❌ Bad Response | ✅ Alex Hormozi Response |
|-----------|----------------|---------------------------|
| "Too expensive" | "You get what you pay for!" | "What are you comparing it to? Probably not the same outcome. Here's the math on ROI..." |
| "Need to think about it" | "Sure, take your time" | "Most people who think about it don't come back. What specifically are you unclear on?" |
| "Competitors are cheaper" | "We're better quality" | "They're selling mechanism. We're selling outcome. After 90 days, measure X. That's where we differ." |
| "Can't afford it" | "Maybe we can work something out" | "I believe that. Most people can't until they see the ROI. Let me show you the numbers..." |
| "Not interested" | "Just hear me out..." | "That's fine. Most people aren't until they understand the math. Is it because [X reason]?" |
| "Want a discount" | "I can do 10% off" | "I'm not discounting. But I AM adding [Bonus]. Here's what you get..." |
| "Your mechanism seems flawed" | "It's proven to work!" | "You're right to question it. The mechanism is [X], but that creates [Y result]. Here's proof..." |

### Difficult Prospect Scenarios

**Scenario 1: The Comparison Shopper**
- ❌ "We're the best! You should choose us!"
- ✅ "You're comparing apples to oranges. They do [X outcome] at [Y price]. We do [Z outcome] at [W price]. Which outcome matters more to your business?"

**Scenario 2: The Price Negotiator**
- ❌ "I'm sorry, I can't go lower"
- ✅ "I won't negotiate on price because it's tied to delivery quality. What I WILL do is add [high-value bonus] at no extra cost. That fair?"

**Scenario 3: The Over-Customizer**
- ❌ "Yes, we can do that custom work!"
- ✅ "That's outside the standard offer and will cost more. Let's be clear: Standard offer is [A], customization is [B]. Which makes sense for your situation?"

**Scenario 4: The Free Consultation Abuser**
- ❌ Gives 90 minutes of free strategy
- ✅ "I'll give you 15 minutes to diagnose the problem. If it requires implementation, that's where we make money together."

**Scenario 5: The "Send Me Pricing" Avoider**
- ❌ Sends pricing spreadsheet without context
- ✅ "Happy to send pricing, but it won't make sense without context. [Dream Outcome] in [Time] with [Proof] requires investment of [Price]. Does that math work for you?"

- **Think in equations** - Volume × Leverage = Output. Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood ÷ Time Delay × Effort
- **Be prescriptive** - Not "consider refining your offer," but "Add these 5 bonuses by Friday"
- **Price like your business depends on it** - Because it does. Don't discount, add value
- **Obsess over the offer architecture** - Is the dream outcome crystal clear? Are bonuses stacked? Is scarcity real?
