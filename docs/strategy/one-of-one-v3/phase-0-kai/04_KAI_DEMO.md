## Demo Format

**WhatsApp-first.** Phone as secondary.

Kai's problem lives on WhatsApp. Teams coordinate shifts, request vacation, and report sick via messaging. The demo meets prospects where the pain is — not on a slide deck, not on a phone call, but in the channel where the chaos actually happens.

---

## Demo Flow

The demo is interactive. The prospect messages Kai on WhatsApp and experiences the product directly. No slides. No screen share (unless requested). Just: "Message this number. Request vacation. See what happens."

### Scenario 1: Vacation Request

**Prospect messages Kai:**
> "Ich moechte vom 14. bis 25. Juli Urlaub nehmen."

**Kai responds:**
- Checks team availability for July 14-25
- Identifies that Lisa Mueller is already off July 14-18 (conflict in Team Nord)
- Shows remaining vacation balance for the requester (18 days remaining, 10 requested)
- Suggests alternative: July 21-August 1 has no conflicts
- Or: approves July 14-25 with a note that coverage is thin July 14-18 and flags it for manager review

**What to highlight:** Kai checked three things instantly that would normally take the owner 15-20 minutes and an Excel file: availability, conflicts, and remaining balance.

### Scenario 2: Sick Call Coverage

**Prospect messages Kai:**
> "Michael ist heute krank. Wer kann seine Schicht uebernehmen?"

**Kai responds:**
- Identifies Michael's shift (today, 08:00-16:00, Team Sued)
- Checks who is available and not already scheduled
- Suggests: "Sarah Braun is available and has covered for Michael before. Thomas Klein is also available but is on a different team."
- Offers to notify Sarah directly: "Shall I message Sarah and ask if she can cover?"

**What to highlight:** Monday morning, 7 AM, someone's sick. Instead of calling 5 people, the owner messages Kai once. Coverage found in 30 seconds.

### Scenario 3: Availability Check

**Prospect messages Kai:**
> "Wer ist naechsten Donnerstag Nachmittag verfuegbar?"

**Kai responds:**
- Pulls real-time availability for Thursday afternoon
- Shows: "Available: Anna Weber, Thomas Klein, Petra Hoffmann. Scheduled: Lisa Mueller (Aussendienst), Michael Braun (Urlaub). On shift: Lukas Schmidt (Fruehschicht bis 14:00, danach verfuegbar)."

**What to highlight:** One question, instant answer. No checking three different calendars, no "let me get back to you."

### Scenario 4: Vacation Balance Inquiry

**Prospect messages Kai:**
> "Wie viele Urlaubstage hat Anna noch?"

**Kai responds:**
- "Anna Weber: 28 Tage Jahresurlaub, 12 genommen, 16 verbleibend. Davon 3 Tage bereits genehmigt fuer September."

**What to highlight:** Instant data that normally lives in a spreadsheet the owner has to open, find, and cross-reference.

## Demo Expansion Moment

After the four scenarios, deliver the platform pivot. This is the moment that separates Kai from a scheduling tool:

> "That's Kai — your team ops coordinator. He handles vacation requests, shift coverage, availability, and conflict resolution. Your team messages him on WhatsApp, and the coordination just happens.
>
> Now imagine: Clara answers every phone call your business gets — qualifies the caller, books appointments, routes inquiries. Maren coordinates scheduling across your locations. Jonas qualifies incoming leads and routes them to the right person. Tobias turns voice notes from the field into formatted quotes. Lina follows up with every customer automatically via WhatsApp and email. Nora gives you real-time performance visibility across your entire operation.
>
> Seven specialists. One platform. Kai is just the beginning."

Do not oversell. Let the prospect sit with it. If they ask about Clara or another agent, that's the expansion signal. Note it, follow up after Kai is live.

## Why WhatsApp-First

| Reason | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Meets the pain where it lives** | The problem is on WhatsApp. The solution should be too. |
| **Lower barrier than phone** | People are more comfortable messaging than calling. No anxiety about "talking to an AI." |
| **Shows multimodal capability** | The platform isn't just voice. WhatsApp today, phone tomorrow, email next. |
| **Instant shareability** | Prospect can forward the WhatsApp number to a colleague: "Try this." Viral potential within the company. |
| **Team adoption starts at demo** | If the prospect messages Kai and finds it useful, their team will too. The demo IS the onboarding. |

## What the Demo Must NOT Do

- Do not position Kai as an "HR tool" or "scheduling software." Kai is a team ops coordinator — part of a 7-agent platform.
- Do not show a dashboard or admin panel in Phase 0. The product is the conversation. The interface is WhatsApp.
- Do not demo features that don't exist yet. If approval workflows aren't built, don't promise them. Show what works.
- Do not let the demo run longer than 10 minutes for the interactive portion. 4 scenarios, expansion pitch, done. Follow-up call for deeper questions.
