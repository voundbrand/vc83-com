## The Pitch

> "How do your people request vacation right now? WhatsApp? Email? A shared spreadsheet? What happens when someone calls in sick on a Monday morning — how many calls do you make to find coverage?
>
> Kai handles all of that. Your team messages Kai to request time off, check availability, swap shifts, or report sick. Kai knows who's available, handles conflicts, and notifies you. No more WhatsApp chaos. No more spreadsheet juggling.
>
> And Kai is just one of seven AI specialists. Once you see what ops automation looks like, we can expand into call handling, scheduling, follow-up, and more."

This pitch works because:
1. It starts with their reality (WhatsApp, Excel, phone calls).
2. It describes the solution in their terms (messaging, availability, coverage).
3. It closes with the platform tease — Kai is the door, not the house.

## 90-Day Plan

### Week 1-2: Build the Kai Demo

**Objective:** A working Kai demo that prospects can interact with on WhatsApp.

Build:
- Vacation request flow: team member messages Kai, Kai checks availability, confirms or flags conflict
- Shift coverage scenario: someone reports sick, Kai identifies available replacements, notifies them
- Availability check: "Who's available next Thursday afternoon?" — instant answer
- Conflict resolution: two people request the same week off, Kai handles it

Demo requirements:
- Accessible via WhatsApp (Kai's natural channel) — prospect messages a demo number
- Phone as secondary channel (show multimodal capability)
- Pre-loaded with a fictional team of 12 employees (see `04_KAI_DEMO.md` for full spec)
- Show how Kai connects to the rest of the platform (teaser slide for Clara, Maren, etc.)

Testing target: 30+ internal test runs across all scenarios before first prospect demo.

### Week 3-6: Find the Pain (10-15 Conversations)

**Objective:** 10-15 meaningful conversations, 3-5 demo calls scheduled.

Outreach channels:

**BNI and networking groups:**
- Different question than Track A: "How do you handle vacation planning?" instead of "What happens when your phone rings?"
- Listen for: WhatsApp groups, Excel spreadsheets, "I just handle it myself," seasonal stress mentions
- Same rooms, different ears

**LinkedIn (Track B targeting):**
- Target ops managers, HR leads, team coordinators — not just owners
- Content angle: "Your team is running shift coordination on a messaging app designed for birthday party planning."
- Seasonal hook: "Summer's coming. Is your vacation planning sorted, or are you dreading the spreadsheet?"
- Connection requests to: Operations Manager, Teamleiter, Personalleiter, Schichtleiter in target verticals

**Warm network:**
- Ask existing contacts: "Do you know anyone who manages a team of 10+ and handles vacation requests manually?"
- Referral angle differs from Track A — this reaches ops people, not just owners

**Seasonal trigger campaigns (March-June):**
- "Summer vacation season is 8 weeks away. How many conflicts are already brewing?"
- "Last year, how many hours did you spend sorting out summer vacation coverage?"
- Urgency is built-in. This isn't a hypothetical — vacation planning is happening NOW.

Goal: 3-5 demo calls booked by end of week 6.

### Week 5-10: Run Trials (2-3 at a Time)

**Objective:** 2-3 active trials where Kai handles real team coordination for real teams.

Trial structure:
- **Duration:** 2 weeks (same as Track A)
- **Scope:** Kai handles vacation requests and shift coordination for one team or location
- **Setup:** Import team roster, vacation balances, shift schedules. Kai takes over coordination.
- **Channel:** WhatsApp group where team members message Kai directly

This is lower-touch than Track A Clara trials:
- No real-time call monitoring needed
- No call quality review
- Team adoption is measurable by message volume and response rates
- Fewer fire drills (a missed vacation request is annoying; a missed phone call is urgent)

Daily check-in questions:
- "How did the team respond? Is anyone still going to WhatsApp instead of Kai?"
- "Did Kai catch any conflicts you would have missed?"
- "How much time did you personally spend on scheduling today vs. last week?"

Data to collect during trial:
| Metric | What It Proves |
|---|---|
| Requests handled by Kai | Volume / adoption |
| Average response time | Speed vs. manual process |
| Conflicts detected | Value: prevented scheduling disasters |
| Coverage found (sick call scenarios) | Time saved, coverage speed |
| Manual hours saved (self-reported) | ROI for pricing conversation |
| Team adoption rate (% of team using Kai vs. old method) | Stickiness indicator |

### Week 8-13: Convert

**Objective:** Convert 2-3 trials into paying customers.

Close mechanics:
- Phase 0 pricing: EUR 1,500-2,500/month, month-to-month (see `03_KAI_PRICING.md`)
- No setup fee in Phase 0
- Transition: "Your team is already using Kai. Let's keep it running."

Collect at conversion:
- Team adoption data (% of team actively using Kai)
- Time saved metrics (hours/week of manual coordination eliminated)
- Owner/ops manager testimonial (written or video)
- Permission to use company name/logo in case study
- Permission to share data with future prospects

Expansion pitch at close:
> "Now that your team ops are handled, want to hear about Clara? She answers your phone the same way Kai manages your team. Same platform, same quality, another problem solved."

## Solo Founder Time Split (Both Tracks Combined)

Running two tracks on one person requires a rigid weekly rhythm:

| Day | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Monday** | Track A outreach + trial monitoring (Clara/phone wedge) |
| **Tuesday** | Product work (shared platform — benefits both tracks) |
| **Wednesday** | Track B outreach + demo calls (Kai/ops wedge) |
| **Thursday** | Product work + trial check-ins (both tracks) |
| **Friday** | LinkedIn post (alternate A/B messaging week to week) + admin |

Time allocation:
- ~5-6 hours/week selling Track A
- ~5-6 hours/week selling Track B
- ~15-20 hours/week building (shared platform, benefits both tracks)
- ~3-4 hours/week admin, content, follow-up

This is tight. It works because:
1. The platform is shared — building for one track improves both.
2. Outreach is concentrated (one day per track, not scattered).
3. Trials are lower-touch than building (check-ins, not full-time monitoring).

If it becomes clear that one track converts significantly faster, shift time allocation accordingly. The weekly rhythm is a starting framework, not a permanent commitment.

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| **"We already use Personio / HRworks."** | "Great for formal HR processes — contracts, payroll, annual reviews. Kai handles the real-time chaos that Personio doesn't touch: the sick call at 7 AM, the last-minute shift swap, the 'who's actually available Thursday?' question. Personio tracks records. Kai manages the day." |

| **"EUR 2K/month for vacation planning?"** | "It's not just vacation. Kai coordinates shifts, finds coverage, tracks availability, resolves conflicts, and notifies your team — automatically. That's your ops manager's Monday morning, handled. And once you see what AI ops automation does for coordination, there are six more specialists that can handle your phones, scheduling, follow-up, and reporting." |
| **"My team won't adopt it."** | "That's exactly why we run a 2-week trial. If your team doesn't use it, you don't pay. But here's what we see: people prefer messaging an AI that responds in 5 seconds over waiting for their manager to check a spreadsheet and reply in 3 hours." |
| **"I need to think about it."** | "Completely fair. While you think, summer vacation requests are piling up. Can I set up Kai for just one team — 5 people — for two weeks? Zero commitment. If they don't use it, we shake hands and part ways." |
