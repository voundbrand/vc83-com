## One-Sentence Strategy

**Sell the platform through vacation/shift chaos — every business runs team ops on WhatsApp. We replace the chaos with an AI ops coordinator, then expand into the full agent suite.**

---

## Why Kai as a Separate Wedge

Track A enters through the phone. Track B enters through the ops layer. Same platform, different pain, different buyer.

### 1. Seasonal Urgency

Vacation planning pain spikes March-June (summer bookings) and Oct-Nov (year-end use-it-or-lose-it days). We are in March. The pain is live right now. Prospects don't need to imagine the problem — they are living it this week.

### 2. Universal Problem

Not every business has phone pain. Some businesses barely get inbound calls. But every business with 5+ employees has vacation coordination, shift coverage, and availability headaches. The TAM for "team ops chaos" is broader than "missed phone calls."

### 3. Stickier Entry

Once Kai manages vacation requests and shift coverage, the entire team relies on it daily. That's 10-30 people using the product, not just the owner. Harder to cancel than a phone agent that only the owner sees the value of. Team adoption creates structural lock-in.

### 4. Different Buyer

Clara sells to owners who hear the phone ring. Kai sells to ops managers, HR leads, team coordinators, and owners drowning in WhatsApp group coordination. Different title, different pain, different conversation — but the same platform behind it.

## How Kai Differs from Track A

| Dimension | Track A (Clara) | Track B (Kai) |
|---|---|---|
| **Pain** | "Nobody picks up our phone" | "Vacation planning is chaos, shift coverage is manual, nobody knows who's available" |
| **Entry agent** | Clara — Virtual Receptionist | Kai — Team Operations |
| **Primary channel** | Phone (voice) | WhatsApp (messaging) |
| **Buyer** | Owner / front-desk manager | Owner / ops manager / HR lead / team coordinator |
| **Demo moment** | Call the number, hear Clara answer | Message Kai on WhatsApp, request vacation |
| **Value proof** | Missed calls caught, appointments booked | Hours saved, conflicts prevented, coverage found |
| **Stickiness** | Owner sees value | Whole team uses it daily |

---

## Why Run Both in Parallel

Different pain = different prospects. Two wedges = double the surface area.

A BNI contact who says "our phones are fine" might also say "but vacation planning is killing me." That's a Track B prospect. Running both tracks means every conversation has two chances to land.

And the data matters: if one track converts faster, that tells us which door mid-market buyers will walk through in Phase 1. We don't need to guess — we run both and let the results decide.

---

## Critical Note

**Kai is NOT a standalone HR/scheduling SaaS.** It is not Personio. It is not HRworks. It is not a time-tracking tool.

Kai is the same sevenlayers platform entered through a different door. Once a customer has Kai managing their team coordination, Clara, Maren, Lina, Jonas, Tobias, and Nora are natural expansions. The platform is one product. Kai is just the wedge.

Never position Kai as a point solution. Always frame it as the first specialist they meet — with six more behind it.
