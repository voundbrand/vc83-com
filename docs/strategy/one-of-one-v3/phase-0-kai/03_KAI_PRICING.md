## Pricing Philosophy

Same as Track A: earn the right to charge more later.

Phase 0 pricing exists to remove friction, not to maximize revenue. The goal is paying customers, retention data, and case studies. Pricing dignity comes in Phase 1 when those proof artifacts exist.

## Phase 0 Kai Pricing

| Tier | Setup | Monthly | Terms |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Kai Proof Customer** | EUR 0 | EUR 1,500-2,500/mo | Month-to-month |

### Why the Range

- **EUR 1,500/mo** — Smaller teams (5-10 people), single location, basic vacation + shift coordination. Use this floor for businesses where the perceived pain is lower or the buyer needs convincing.
- **EUR 2,500/mo** — Larger teams (15-30 people), multiple shift patterns, higher coordination complexity. Use the ceiling when the pain is obvious and the buyer is already sold on the problem.

### Why Potentially Lower Than Track A

| Factor | Explanation |
|---|---|
| **Perceived value gap** | "Answering every phone call" feels more revenue-critical than "managing vacation requests." Phone = money. Vacation = operations. Owners instinctively value revenue recovery higher. |
| **Faster ROI demonstration** | Time saved is easier to quantify than revenue recovered. "Kai saved you 8 hours this week" lands faster than "Clara might have caught a EUR 5K customer." |
| **Lower switching cost** | Kai replaces WhatsApp and Excel. Clara replaces a phone system or answering service. The thing being replaced has lower perceived value. |
| **Nice-to-have perception** | Some buyers will see vacation planning as operational overhead, not strategic. Price needs to be low enough that the "nice-to-have" objection doesn't stick. |

This is a Phase 0 reality, not a permanent position. As case studies prove ROI and team adoption data shows stickiness, Kai pricing rises.

## Framing for the Pricing Conversation

> "Less than the cost of the hours your ops manager spends on Excel every month. And Kai never takes a vacation."

Supporting math for the conversation:
- Ops manager or owner spends 5-10 hours/week on scheduling coordination
- At EUR 30-50/hour loaded cost, that's EUR 600-2,000/month in time
- Kai costs EUR 1,500-2,500/month but also eliminates errors, conflicts, and coverage gaps
- Net: Kai pays for itself in time savings alone, before counting prevented scheduling disasters

Never lead with this math. Let the prospect do the mental calculation. Ask: "How many hours a week do you spend on vacation and shift coordination?" Then let the silence do the work.

## Expansion Pricing (After Kai Is Sticky)

Once Kai is embedded and the team relies on it, the platform expansion conversation opens naturally.

| Add-On | Monthly | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| **Clara** (phone/virtual receptionist) | +EUR 1,000-1,500/mo | "Now that ops are handled, want to stop missing calls too?" |
| **Maren** (appointment coordination) | +EUR 500-1,000/mo | "Kai manages your team. Maren manages your customer appointments." |
| **Lina** (customer follow-up) | +EUR 500-1,000/mo | "Your team is coordinated. Now let's make sure no customer falls through the cracks." |
| **Full platform** | Transition to v3 pricing tiers | When 3+ agents are active, move to platform pricing |

The expansion motion:
1. Month 1-2: Kai only. Prove value. Build trust.
2. Month 3: "Your team coordination is running smoothly. What about [phones / scheduling / follow-up]?"
3. Month 4+: Second agent added. Customer is now on a multi-agent platform.
4. Phase 1: Transition to v3 platform pricing (see `../05_PRICING_V3.md`).

## When to Raise Kai Pricing

| Milestone | Action |
|---|---|
| **After 3 Kai customers** | Raise floor to EUR 2,500-3,500/month. Three paying references = pricing leverage. |
| **After 5 Kai customers** | Introduce setup fee: EUR 3,000-5,000 (team onboarding, policy configuration, integration setup). |
| **Phase 1 transition** | Kai becomes part of standard platform pricing. No more standalone Kai deals. New customers buy the platform. |

---

## Pricing Principles (Shared with Track A)

1. **Never discount below the floor.** EUR 1,500 is the absolute minimum. Below that, the customer doesn't value it enough to be a useful proof customer.
2. **Month-to-month only in Phase 0.** No annual contracts. If they stay, it's because the product works. That's the data we need.
3. **No free trials beyond 2 weeks.** The trial is free. After that, they're paying or they're gone. Extended free periods attract tire-kickers, not customers.
4. **Price in value, not features.** "EUR 2K/month to eliminate scheduling chaos" not "EUR 2K/month for an AI WhatsApp bot."
5. **The expansion upsell is the real pricing strategy.** Kai at EUR 2K is the foot in the door. The platform at EUR 5-10K is where the business model lives.
