## Kai Phase 0 ICP

**Any business with 5+ employees that coordinates schedules manually.**

No vertical restriction. Vacation chaos, shift coverage, and availability confusion are universal. The ICP is defined by the coordination pain, not the industry.

---

## Where Pain Is Worst

While any business with 5+ people qualifies, some verticals feel the pain harder:

### Service Businesses with Shifts
- Restaurants, cafes, bars (daily shift coverage, weekend/holiday planning)
- Retail stores (floor coverage, seasonal staffing spikes)
- Medical/dental clinics (provider scheduling, vacation coverage for patient continuity)
- Hotels, hospitality (front desk, housekeeping, F&B shift coordination)

### Trades and Field Services
- Electrical, plumbing, HVAC companies (field crew scheduling, availability across jobs)
- Construction firms (project-based teams, vacation conflicts across job sites)
- Cleaning companies (route and shift coverage when someone is sick)

### Seasonal Businesses
- Tourism, outdoor recreation, event companies (summer staff surges, vacation blackout conflicts)
- Landscaping, gardening services (peak season = nobody can take vacation = resentment)

### Professional Services with Teams
- Agencies (project staffing, handover coverage during vacation)
- Accounting firms (busy season scheduling, year-end vacation crunches)
- Law firms with support staff (coverage coordination, availability tracking)

## Decision-Maker

The buyer is the person currently acting as the "human scheduler" — whoever fields vacation requests, finds shift coverage, and tracks who is available.

| Title | Why They Buy |
|---|---|
| **Owner** | Tired of being the default scheduler. Vacation requests land in their inbox. Sick calls come to their phone. |
| **Ops Manager** | This is literally their job, and they're doing it on WhatsApp and Excel. Kai automates 60-80% of their coordination load. |
| **Team Lead / Shift Lead** | Responsible for coverage but has no tools. Currently calls people one by one when someone calls in sick. |
| **HR Lead** (if they have one) | Manages vacation balances, approval workflows, and compliance — all manually. |

In Phase 0 SMBs (5-30 employees), the owner and the scheduler are usually the same person. That is ideal: one decision-maker who both feels the pain and signs the check.

## Pain Signals That Predict a Close

Ranked by strength of buying intent:

### Tier 1 — Strong Signals (Prospect Is Ready)

1. **"We use a WhatsApp group for shift changes."**
   Manual, chaotic, no audit trail, messages get lost, people miss notifications. This is the #1 signal because it means they have the pain AND no solution.

2. **"Vacation requests are on paper / email / Excel."**
   Owner spends hours reconciling, checking for conflicts, tracking remaining days. One spreadsheet error and two people are off the same week.

3. **"When someone calls in sick, I have to call 5 people to find coverage."**
   The Monday morning scramble. Owner becomes a switchboard operator for their own team. Revenue-impacting if the shift goes uncovered.

### Tier 2 — Medium Signals (Pain Exists, May Need Education)

4. **"I don't know who's available next week without checking 3 systems."**
   Fragmented data: calendar, spreadsheet, WhatsApp, memory. No single source of truth for team availability.

5. **"We've had scheduling conflicts that cost us customers."**
   Revenue impact from understaffing. Two people off the same week, no one to cover a client meeting, a shift that went empty. This signal connects ops pain to money.

### Tier 3 — Soft Signals (Worth Exploring)

6. **"Summer vacation planning is always stressful."**
   Seasonal pain. May not feel urgent in November, but in March-June it is top of mind.

7. **"I wish I had better visibility into my team's schedule."**
   Desire for a dashboard or overview. Kai provides this as a side effect.

## What Makes a GOOD Kai Phase 0 Customer

| Criterion | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Currently uses WhatsApp / paper / Excel for coordination | Low bar to beat. Any automation is an upgrade. |
| 5-30 employees | Enough complexity to need Kai, small enough for fast decisions. |
| Owner or ops manager decides directly | No committee, no procurement, no legal review. |
| Feels the pain personally | The buyer is the one fielding WhatsApp messages at 7 AM. |
| Open to trying something new for 2 weeks | Willingness to run a trial with one team or location. |
| Would tell another business owner about it | Referral potential for Phase 0 growth. |

---

## Disqualification Criteria

Only disqualify if:

| Reason | Why |
|---|---|
| **Fewer than 3 employees** | No scheduling complexity. Two people can coordinate over lunch. |
| **Already fully digital** | Using Personio, HRworks, Factorial, or similar with high adoption. Kai adds less value when the problem is already solved. Note: "we have Personio but nobody uses it" is still qualified. |
| **No decision-maker access** | If the person with the pain cannot sign a contract, the sales cycle becomes enterprise-length. Not Phase 0. |
| **Multi-month procurement** | Any business requiring legal review, IT security audit, or committee approval for a EUR 2K/month tool. Not Phase 0. |
