## Three Target Verticals

### 1. Medical Groups / MVZ (Medizinische Versorgungszentren)

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Why this vertical** | 5–20 locations, high phone volume, patient scheduling pain, GDPR sensitivity (sells sovereignty modules), budget at group management level |
| **Core pain** | Each location misses 15–30% of patient calls. Across 10 locations = hundreds of lost appointments per month |
| **Entry motion** | Phone agent at one location for 2 weeks. Measure missed calls before/after |
| **Expansion path** | Multi-location rollout, patient scheduling across locations, data sovereignty module |
| **Deal size** | €35K–€100K initial, €195K+ with sovereignty |
| **Where to find them** | KV (Kassenärztliche Vereinigung) events, healthcare LinkedIn, medical practice management conferences |

**Why MVZ specifically:** Group-managed medical practices are a consolidating market in Germany. Private equity is buying up dental, orthopaedic, and ophthalmology practices. The new group owners are operations-minded, not physicians — they think in missed calls, utilisation rates, and revenue per location. They have budget, they have pain, and they have the authority to sign.

---

### 2. Real Estate Agencies (Makler-Ketten)

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Why this vertical** | 10–50 agents, high phone volume, competitive market (speed to answer = deal won), already have Marcus Engel case study |
| **Core pain** | Each agent misses 25–40% of calls. Scale Marcus Engel's story across 10+ agents |
| **Entry motion** | Phone agent for one office/team for 2 weeks |
| **Expansion path** | All offices, CRM integration, multi-channel (WhatsApp, email), listing knowledge |
| **Deal size** | €35K–€80K initial |
| **Where to find them** | IVD (Immobilienverband Deutschland), real estate industry events, LinkedIn |

**Why real estate specifically:** The Marcus Engel case study is proof. Real estate is a speed game — the first agent to call back wins the listing or the buyer. Every missed call is a lost commission worth thousands. Agency owners already understand ROI math because they live it every day. And the competitive pressure means they'll move fast.

---

### 3. Mittelstand Service Companies

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Why this vertical** | Electrical contractors, HVAC, facility management with 50+ employees across regions. Field workers can't answer phones. Dispatch/coordination chaos |
| **Core pain** | Missed customer calls, slow quote turnaround, coordination across field teams |
| **Entry motion** | Phone agent at HQ for 2 weeks |
| **Expansion path** | Per-region deployment, quote automation (voice-to-quote) |
| **Deal size** | €35K–€100K |
| **Where to find them** | Handwerkskammer events, IHK, industry trade fairs, BNI introductions |

**Why Mittelstand service companies specifically:** These businesses are built on reputation and responsiveness. A plumbing emergency at 7 AM that goes to voicemail means the customer calls the next company on Google. The owner knows this — they used to answer the phone themselves. Now they have 80 employees across three regions and nobody picks up. The pain is visceral. The demo hits immediately.

## Disqualification Criteria

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Under 50 employees | Redirect to Foundation tier |
| Under €5M revenue | Redirect to Foundation tier |
| Single location | Redirect to Foundation tier |
| No phone volume | Disqualify — not our ICP |
| Purely online business | Disqualify — no phone pain |
| Procurement-heavy enterprise | Disqualify — too slow for founder-led sales |

> If they don't have a phone ringing that nobody answers, we don't have a demo that sells itself. Move on.

## Secondary ICP: Foundation Overflow

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Profile** | Businesses that find us through the landing page but are too small for mid-market |
| **Value** | Still valuable at €7K + €499/mo |
| **Role** | The landing page serves both — mid-market as primary, Foundation as catch-all |

Foundation overflow is not waste. These are paying customers who fund operational costs and validate the product in new use cases. They just aren't where the growth math lives.
