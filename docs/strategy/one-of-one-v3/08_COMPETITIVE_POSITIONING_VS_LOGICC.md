# One-of-One Strategy v3 — Competitive Positioning vs Logicc

|-------|-------|

| **Date** | 2026-03-13 |

---

## Purpose

This document defines how we position against Logicc without getting dragged into the wrong category.

The goal is not to "beat Logicc at AI software." The goal is to win deals where the buyer has a revenue-critical operations problem that a horizontal AI workspace does not solve.

---

## Executive Verdict

**Can we compete with Logicc?** Yes, but only if we refuse a head-to-head fight in the horizontal AI workspace category.

If we market ourselves as:

- a secure AI platform,
- a team AI workspace,
- a central place to use multiple models,
- a general assistant for knowledge workers,

then we will be compared on seat price, breadth of models, collaboration UX, integrations, and self-serve onboarding. That is Logicc's game, not ours.

If we market ourselves as:

- the intelligent front door for multi-location businesses,
- the system that answers every call and captures every lead,
- a 2-week proof that shows recovered revenue with real call data,
- founder-led deployment for operations teams with real rollout complexity,

then we are not really in the same category.

---

## What Logicc Appears to Sell

As of March 13, 2026, Logicc's public positioning appears to be:

- a European, DSGVO-focused AI workspace,
- access to multiple leading models in one product,
- assistants for teams and departments,
- transparent per-user pricing,
- product-led adoption with free trial and low-friction signup,
- enterprise expansion via compliance, governance, and workspace controls.

### The important implication

Logicc is optimized for **internal knowledge work adoption**.

Their buyer is likely some mix of:

- founders,
- team leads,
- office teams,
- knowledge workers,
- innovation or digitalization owners.

Their product pitch is fundamentally:

> "Use AI across your company in one secure place."

That is broad, useful, and dangerous if we let ourselves sound similar.

---

## What We Must Sell Instead

Our wedge is narrower and more valuable.

We sell:

- one public entry point for the business,
- one coherent customer-facing experience,
- multiple hidden specialist agents underneath,
- deployment into live operational workflows,
- measurable impact on answer rate, response time, bookings, and routing,
- expansion across locations after proof at one location.

Our product pitch is fundamentally:

> "We stop revenue leakage at the operational front door, prove it in 14 days, and then scale it across locations."

That is not a better chat app. It is operational infrastructure.

---

## Where We Lose

We lose if the prospect wants:

- an all-purpose internal AI tool for the whole company,
- seat-based pricing,
- broad model choice as the main value proposition,
- self-serve rollout with minimal services,
- a generic "secure ChatGPT for teams" replacement.

If that is the buying intent, we should not try to out-pitch Logicc. We should disqualify or reposition.

---

## Where We Win

We win when the prospect has:

- 3 to 20 locations,
- missed inbound calls,
- slow response times,
- appointment or lead loss tied to availability,
- frontline coordination problems,
- a management team that can approve a deployment based on ROI.

We win especially hard when:

- the phone is still the front door,
- location variation matters,
- a call center exists but performs poorly,
- compliance or German hosting matters,
- the buyer needs proof before internal rollout.

In that context, a horizontal AI workspace is adjacent software. It is not the answer.

---

## Category Discipline

This is the rule:

**Never let the market place us in "general AI software."**

Always pull the frame back to:

1. missed calls,
2. missed appointments,
3. fragmented frontline operations,
4. one number and one customer-facing experience,
5. proof in 14 days,
6. scale across locations.

### Phrases to avoid

- "all your AI in one place"
- "central AI platform"
- "AI workspace for your team"
- "secure access to the best models"
- "assistant for every employee"

These phrases push us into Logicc's category.

### Phrases to repeat

- "one intelligent front door for the business"
- "every missed call costs you a customer"
- "real calls, real data, real results"
- "prove it at one location, then scale"
- "one visible number, multiple hidden specialists"

These phrases keep us in our own category.

---

## Positioning Line for Sales Calls

Use this when a buyer already knows Logicc or another AI workspace:

> "Logicc is a good fit if your goal is giving internal teams a secure place to use AI. That is not what we sell. We fix the operational front door of the business: calls answered, appointments booked, leads routed, response time collapsed from hours to minutes. Keep the workspace tool if you want it. We are the layer that prevents revenue from leaking before your team even gets involved."

This answer does three things:

1. shows confidence,
2. avoids a commodity feature battle,
3. makes coexistence possible instead of forcing a false replacement story.

---

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We already use Logicc." | "Fine. Keep it for internal AI usage. We are not replacing your team workspace. We are solving missed inbound revenue and operational coverage." |
| "Why not use Logicc for this too?" | "Because the problem is not access to models. The problem is deployment into your live phone, routing, scheduling, escalation, and reporting workflows with measurable location-level ROI." |
| "This sounds like another AI platform." | "No. Platforms sell possibility. We sell a live operational outcome: fewer missed calls, more bookings, faster response times, and a rollout model that proves itself in two weeks." |
| "Why is this not priced per seat?" | "Because the value is not tied to employee usage. It is tied to business coverage and recovered revenue at the point customers contact you." |

---

## What Must Exist for This Positioning to Hold

This strategy only works if we have proof, not just framing.

Required proof assets:

- one flagship case study with hard before/after phone metrics,
- one vertical-specific ROI calculator,
- one live demo number that works flawlessly,
- one 2-week trial dashboard that is easy to present,
- one concise explanation of German hosting, GDPR posture, and self-hosted option.

If these do not exist, the positioning will collapse back into generic AI claims.

---

## Strategic Rule

Do not ask:

> "How do we beat Logicc?"

Ask:

> "How do we make Logicc irrelevant in the deals we actually want?"

The answer is:

- pick a painful operational wedge,
- prove it with live trials,
- speak in lost revenue and coverage,
- scale through location rollouts,
- treat horizontal AI workspaces as adjacent, not primary competition.

---

## Final Decision

**We can compete with Logicc if we stay in the AI operator category.**

**We should not compete with Logicc as a horizontal AI workspace.**

That means:

- no generic AI-platform messaging,
- no seat-pricing story,
- no "best models in one app" pitch,
- no broad knowledge-worker positioning.

It means:

- phone-led demo,
- multi-location ROI,
- operational proof,
- founder-led close,
- expansion after trial.

That is the lane.
