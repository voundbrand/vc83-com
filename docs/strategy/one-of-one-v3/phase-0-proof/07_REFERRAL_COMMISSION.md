## One-Sentence Strategy

**Your friends' contact lists are your sales team. Pay them well enough to remember you, not so much that you can't eat.**

## The Commission Structure

### 20% of the first 3 months' revenue. Paid as the customer pays.

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Commission rate** | 20% of monthly invoice |
| **Duration** | First 3 months of a paying customer |
| **Payout timing** | Paid after each customer payment clears |
| **Trigger** | Customer signs and pays first invoice |
| **Cap** | 3 months per referred customer, then commission stops |

### Example Payouts

| Customer monthly rate | Month 1 | Month 2 | Month 3 | Total payout |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| €2,000/mo | €400 | €400 | €400 | **€1,200** |
| €2,500/mo | €500 | €500 | €500 | **€1,500** |
| €3,000/mo | €600 | €600 | €600 | **€1,800** |

### Early Churn Protection

Commission tracks the customer, not the calendar. If a referred customer churns after month 1, the referrer gets paid for one month only. No advance payouts, no clawbacks needed — the structure handles it naturally.

| Scenario | Referrer receives |
|---|---|
| Customer pays 3+ months | Full commission (3 months × 20%) |
| Customer churns after month 2 | 2 months × 20% |
| Customer churns after month 1 | 1 month × 20% |
| Customer never pays after trial | €0 |

## Why 20% × 3 Months

### Why not first month only?

€400–600 for an introduction is forgettable. People won't actively think about who in their network needs this. The commission has to be large enough that when they're at dinner with a business owner friend, they remember you exist.

### Why not 6 or 12 months?

At 5 customers all from referrals (worst case), 20% × 12 months = €30,000+ in commission on a business doing €150K ARR with zero runway. That's 20% of gross revenue gone before infrastructure, tools, or food. Unsustainable at Phase 0.

### Why not a flat fee?

"20% Provision" is a story. "€1,000 Vermittlungsprämie" is a transaction. The percentage creates the feeling that they're participating in something — a cut of something real. It also scales naturally: if a customer pays €3K instead of €2K, the referrer benefits too. Alignment.

### The math at full Phase 0 capacity

| Scenario | Customers | All from referrals? | Total commission (3 months) | As % of Year 1 revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 5 at €2K | Yes (worst case) | €6,000 | 5% |
| Target | 5 at €2.5K | 3 from referrals | €4,500 | 3% |
| Optimistic | 5 at €3K | 2 from referrals | €3,600 | 2% |

At 2–5% of Year 1 revenue, the commission structure is cheap for what it buys: warm introductions to decision-makers you'd never reach cold.

## Two Referrer Profiles

Not everyone who reads the DM will respond the same way. There are two types.

### Profile A: The Casual Referrer

> "Oh, my friend Marcus has a dental practice — he complains about missed calls all the time."

**What they do:** Send you one name, maybe make an introduction via WhatsApp, then forget about it.

**What they need to hear:**
> "Schick mir den Namen oder stell mich kurz vor. Wenn er Kunde wird, bekommst du 20 % auf die ersten drei Monate — das sind rund 1.500 Euro. Ich mach den Rest."

**Commission expectation:** One referral, one payout. Low maintenance.

**How to handle:**
- Thank them immediately when they send the name
- Keep them updated: "Hab mit Marcus telefoniert, Demo läuft nächste Woche"
- Pay fast when the customer signs — within 7 days of first customer payment
- Send a short message when each commission payment goes out

Fast, transparent payouts turn casual referrers into repeat referrers.

### Profile B: The Active Seller

> "I know a lot of business owners. How does this work — can I sell it?"

**What they do:** Actively pitch to multiple contacts. Want to understand the product, the pricing, the objections. Treat this like a side hustle.

**What they need to hear:**
> "Gleiche Struktur: 20 Prozent auf die ersten drei Monate pro Kunde. Du bringst mir drei Kunden, das sind 4.500 Euro. Kein Arbeitsvertrag, kein Aufwand. Du machst die Vorstellung, ich mache den Rest."

**Commission expectation:** Multiple referrals, recurring payouts. Wants to know the pipeline.

**How to handle:**
- Give them the DM script (from `06_PITCH_SCRIPTS.md`) to forward or adapt
- Give them the demo number so they can experience it themselves first
- Brief them on the three pain signals: missed calls, scheduling chaos, slow follow-up
- Weekly check-in if they're actively working leads
- Consider a shared WhatsApp group or simple tracker if volume warrants it

Active sellers are rare and valuable. If someone emerges in Phase 0, invest time in them — they're your proto-sales team.

## What a Referrer Needs to Sell

Don't expect referrers to pitch the product. Their job is to make the introduction. Your job is to close.

### The minimum a referrer needs:

1. **The DM script** — ready to forward (from `06_PITCH_SCRIPTS.md`)
2. **The demo number** — so they can say "just call this"
3. **One sentence they can say in person:** "Mein Kumpel hat ein KI-Team gebaut, das dein Büro führt. Ruf mal diese Nummer an — du wirst es nicht glauben."
4. **The URL:** sevenlayers.io

### What they do NOT need:

- Pricing details (you handle that in the demo call)
- Technical explanation (the demo handles that)
- Slide decks, PDFs, or feature lists (the product sells itself)
- A formal agreement (keep it casual in Phase 0 — see Legal section below)

The referrer's only job: get the right person to call the demo number or connect them to Remington. Everything after that is founder-led.

## Commission Communication

### In the DM (simple):

> Empfehlungen: 20 % Provision. Kein Spaß.

### When someone asks "20% wovon?":

> "20 Prozent auf die ersten drei Monate. Bei 2.500 im Monat sind das 1.500 Euro für dich — dafür, dass du mir jemanden vorstellst. Ausgezahlt wird, sobald der Kunde zahlt."

### When someone asks for more details:

> "Ganz einfach: Du stellst mich vor. Ich mache Demo, Trial, Onboarding — alles. Wenn der Kunde unterschreibt, bekommst du 20 Prozent auf die ersten drei Monatszahlungen. Kein Vertrag, kein Kleingedrucktes. Ich schick dir das Geld, sobald die Zahlung eingeht."

### When someone asks "what if they stay longer than 3 months?":

> "Dann hast du mir einen guten Kunden gebracht und ich bin dir dankbar. Die Provision läuft drei Monate. Danach ist es mein Kunde — und du kannst mir den nächsten bringen."

## Tracking

Phase 0 is 5 customers. This does not need a CRM.

### Simple tracking method:

A note or spreadsheet with:

| Referrer | Lead name | Intro date | Demo date | Trial start | Converted? | Month 1 paid | Month 2 paid | Month 3 paid |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Max Müller | Dr. Schmidt | 2026-03-18 | 2026-03-22 | 2026-03-25 | Yes | €500 ✓ | — | — |

Update it when events happen. Share payout status with the referrer proactively — don't make them ask.

## Legal (Phase 0 — Keep It Light)

Phase 0 referral agreements should be informal. A WhatsApp message confirming the terms is enough:

> "Hey [Name], kurz zur Bestätigung: Wenn [Lead] Kunde wird, bekommst du 20 % auf die ersten drei Monatszahlungen. Ich schick dir das Geld jeweils innerhalb von 7 Tagen nach Zahlungseingang. Deal?"

This creates a written record without the friction of a formal contract. At Phase 0 scale (5 customers, 2–5 referrers), handshake agreements with a WhatsApp trail are sufficient.

### When to formalize:

- Phase 1 (5+ customers): Create a simple 1-page referral agreement template
- If an active seller brings 3+ leads: Offer a simple partnership agreement
- If anyone asks for a formal contract: Provide one — never refuse, it shows professionalism

## Phase Transitions

### Phase 0 → Phase 1 (after 5 customers)

| Dimension | Phase 0 | Phase 1 |
|---|---|---|
| **Commission base** | Monthly revenue only (no setup fee) | Setup fee + monthly revenue |
| **Rate** | 20% × 3 months | 15% of setup + 10% × 3 months recurring |
| **Example payout** | €1,500 on a €2.5K/mo customer | €1,500 setup + €750 recurring = €2,250 on a €10K setup + €2.5K/mo customer |
| **Formality** | WhatsApp confirmation | 1-page referral agreement |

### Phase 1 → Phase 2 (after 15 customers)

| Dimension | Phase 1 | Phase 2 |
|---|---|---|
| **Commission base** | Setup + 3 months recurring | Setup only (or tiered by deal size) |
| **Rate** | 15% setup + 10% × 3 months | 10–15% of setup fee |
| **Example payout** | €2,250 on a €10K + €2.5K/mo deal | €3,500–5,250 on a €35K setup deal |
| **Formality** | 1-page agreement | Formal partner agreement with terms |

The commission percentage drops as deal size grows. A 10% referral fee on a €35K setup = €3,500 — more money per deal even at a lower rate. The structure rewards the referrer more while costing you less as a percentage.

## Rules

1. **Pay fast.** Within 7 days of customer payment clearing. Slow payouts kill referral motivation faster than anything.
2. **Pay transparently.** Tell the referrer when the customer signed, when payment came in, and when their commission is on the way.
3. **Never negotiate the rate down.** 20% × 3 months is the deal. If you can't afford it, you can't afford the customer acquisition cost of doing it yourself either.
4. **Double introductions.** If two people refer the same lead, the first introduction wins. Communicate this clearly if it ever comes up.
5. **No commission on self-sourced leads.** If someone refers a lead you were already talking to, no commission. Handle with honesty: "Ich war schon mit denen im Gespräch — aber danke fürs Mitdenken."
6. **Referrer ≠ salesperson.** The referrer makes the introduction. You do the demo, the trial, the close, and the support. If a referrer starts making promises about features or pricing, correct it immediately.
