## Phase 0 ICP

**Any business with 3+ employees, a phone that rings, and a decision-maker reachable in one call.**

No revenue floor. No employee minimum. No vertical restriction.

That's it. If they have phone-based pain and can say yes without a committee, they're a Phase 0 customer.

---

## Niche-Agnostic Philosophy

At zero customers, turning away a willing buyer because they're in the "wrong" vertical is insane. Take anyone with the pain. Let the data tell you which vertical to double down on in Phase 1.

The v3 strategy identifies specific verticals (dental, trades, real estate, medical). Those are hypotheses. Phase 0 tests them by selling to whoever says yes. If 3 of 5 customers are dental practices, that's the vertical signal. If they're spread across 5 industries, that tells you something different. Either way, you learn more from 5 real deployments than from 50 hours of market research.

The vertical strategy crystallizes after Phase 0, not before it.

## Pain Signals That Predict a Close

Ranked by close probability — the higher on this list, the faster they buy:

### 1. "Nobody picks up our phone."
Missed calls are visible and painful. They know they're losing business. Clara solves this on day one. Fastest close in the playbook.

### 2. "I still answer the phone myself."
Owner trapped in operations. They started the business to do the work, not answer phones. Clara gives them their time back. Emotional close.

### 3. "We lose customers to competitors who respond faster."
They've lost deals because someone else picked up first. Speed-to-response is a competitive advantage they can feel. Clara + Jonas + Lina create that speed.

### 4. "Scheduling is chaos."
Manual coordination across people, locations, or calendars. Double-bookings, missed appointments, constant back-and-forth. Maren eliminates this.

### 5. "We have no idea what happens after the first call."
Leads come in, but follow-up is inconsistent. No system, no visibility. Lina + Nora close this gap.

## What Makes a GOOD Phase 0 Customer

- **Can decide in one meeting.** Owner-operator or managing director who doesn't need board approval.
- **No procurement process.** No RFP, no vendor review, no 6-month evaluation cycle.
- **No legal review.** GDPR compliance is built in (EU-hosted, German-language-first), but they shouldn't need a legal team to evaluate a €2K/month service.
- **Values speed over process.** They want the problem solved this month, not evaluated this quarter.
- **Accessible.** Reachable by phone, WhatsApp, or LinkedIn. If you can't get them on a call within a week, move on.
- **Has real pain.** At least one of the five pain signals above is present and acknowledged.

The ideal Phase 0 customer says: "If this works, I'll pay you tomorrow."

---

## Phase 0 Customers Are NOT the v3 ICP

This is important to internalize. Phase 0 customers are:

| Phase 0 | v3 Mid-Market |
|---|---|
| 3-20 employees | 50-500 employees |
| €0-5M revenue | €5-100M revenue |
| Owner decides alone | Buying committee involved |
| Month-to-month at €2-3K | Annual contracts at €35K-€195K |
| No procurement | Formal vendor evaluation |
| Founder-led support | Customer success team |

**That's the point.** Phase 0 customers are easy to close, fast to deploy, and generate proof. They are stepping stones, not the destination. Every Phase 0 customer makes the v3 customer more achievable.

---

## Disqualification Criteria

Disqualify only if:

- **Genuinely no phone/ops pain.** If their front office runs smoothly and they have no coordination overhead, the platform solves a problem they don't have. Don't force it.
- **Decision-maker unreachable.** If you can't get the person who can say yes on a call within 7 days, the opportunity cost is too high at Phase 0 velocity.
- **Expects free indefinitely.** The 2-week trial is free. After that, it's €2-3K/month. If they won't pay after seeing it work, they're not a customer.
- **Needs heavy customization before trial.** Phase 0 runs one universal demo agent. If they need deep custom work before they'll even try it, save them for Phase 1.

Everything else is a go. Weird vertical? Fine. Tiny company? Fine. Non-obvious use case? Even better — you might discover something.

## How Phase 0 Customers Feed v3

Every Phase 0 deployment generates:

1. **Quantitative data** — Calls answered, response times, appointments booked, leads qualified. This becomes the "before/after" in case studies.
2. **Qualitative signal** — Which agents they use most, which features they ask for, what language they use to describe value. This refines the v3 pitch.
3. **Testimonial potential** — A real business owner saying "sevenlayers changed how we operate" is worth more than any slide deck.
4. **Vertical intelligence** — If 3 customers are in the same industry, you have a vertical. If they're spread out, you have horizontal proof.
5. **Objection data** — Every objection heard in Phase 0 becomes an objection pre-handled in Phase 1.

Phase 0 customers are not charity cases getting a discount. They're investors paying below market rate in exchange for providing the proof that funds the entire v3 strategy.
