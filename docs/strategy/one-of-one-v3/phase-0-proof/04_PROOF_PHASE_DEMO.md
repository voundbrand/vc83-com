## Demo Agent: "Schmitt & Partner"

A fictional multi-service business designed to be relatable across verticals.

**Why "Schmitt & Partner":**
- Generic enough to map onto any business type (dental practice, plumbing company, real estate agency, law firm, salon).
- Specific enough to feel real (takes service calls, books appointments, handles inquiries, coordinates a team).
- German name signals DACH market focus without limiting appeal.
- "& Partner" implies a team, which makes all 7 agents feel natural.

**Demo business profile:**
- Multi-service business with 8 employees
- Two locations (main office + satellite)
- Takes 30-50 calls per day
- Mix of new inquiries, existing customer calls, and internal coordination
- Typical German Mittelstand business with real operational complexity

**Availability:** 24/7 on demo number. If someone calls at 2am on a Sunday, Clara answers. This is the single most impressive thing about the demo — it never sleeps, never takes a break, never has a bad day.

## Demo Flow

The demo is a single phone call that showcases all 7 agents. The caller drives the conversation; the agents respond naturally.

### 1. Clara — Virtual Receptionist
**Trigger:** Call the number.
Clara answers warmly, identifies herself as the receptionist for Schmitt & Partner, and asks how she can help. She handles the initial greeting, determines intent, and routes accordingly.

### 2. Maren — Appointment Coordinator
**Trigger:** "I'd like to make an appointment" or "When are you available?"
Maren takes over scheduling. She checks availability, proposes times, confirms location, and books the appointment. She handles rescheduling and cancellation requests naturally.

### 3. Jonas — Lead Qualification
**Trigger:** "I need a quote for..." or describe a service need.
Jonas qualifies the inquiry: what service, what scope, what timeline, what budget range. He routes the qualified lead appropriately and confirms next steps.

### 4. Lina — Customer Follow-Up
**Trigger:** "What happens after I book?" or "Will someone follow up?"
Lina explains the follow-up process: confirmation via WhatsApp/email, reminder before the appointment, satisfaction check after the service. She demonstrates the engagement loop.

### 5. Kai — Team Operations
**Trigger:** "Can I speak to someone about team scheduling?" or "How do you handle coverage?"
Kai explains how team coordination works: shift scheduling, vacation coverage, task assignments. He demonstrates the operations layer that keeps the team running.

### 6. Tobias — Field Documentation
**Trigger:** "How do you handle quotes in the field?" or "What about documentation?"
Tobias explains how field workers capture notes, generate quotes, and document work — all through voice. Voice notes become formatted documents without manual data entry.

### 7. Nora — Location Intelligence
**Trigger:** "How do I know what's happening across locations?" or "What kind of reporting do you have?"
Nora provides real-time visibility: call volumes, response times, conversion rates, team utilization. She demonstrates the analytics layer that turns operations into insights.

## Why One Universal Demo

### Vertical-specific demos are Phase 1.
Building a dental demo, a plumbing demo, a real estate demo, and a law firm demo before having a single customer in any of those verticals is premature optimization. It costs time that should go to conversations and trials.

### Phase 0 needs data to specialize.
After 5 deployments, patterns emerge: which agents get used most, which pain points resonate, which verticals close. That data — not strategy docs — determines which vertical-specific demos to build in Phase 1.

### "Schmitt & Partner" is universally relatable.
Whether the prospect runs a dental practice, plumbing company, real estate agency, or law firm, they recognize the scenario: phone rings, appointments need scheduling, leads need qualifying, team needs coordinating. The specific industry doesn't matter — the operational pain is the same.

### Same demo, different emphasis.
The demo flow covers all 7 agents, but in conversation, lean into what resonates:
- Dental practice worried about missed calls? Lead with Clara.
- Trades company drowning in scheduling? Lead with Maren.
- Real estate agency losing leads to slow follow-up? Lead with Lina + Jonas.
- Growing team struggling with coordination? Lead with Kai.

The demo is the same. The story around it adapts to the pain.

## Demo Quality Standards

Before going live with the demo number:

- [ ] Clara handles 50+ test calls without breaking
- [ ] Each agent handoff is smooth and natural
- [ ] German language quality is native-level (no awkward phrasing, no English leakage)
- [ ] Handles unexpected questions gracefully (doesn't hallucinate, doesn't freeze)
- [ ] Call quality is clear (no latency issues, no audio artifacts)
- [ ] Available 24/7 with no downtime during test period

The demo is the product in Phase 0. If the demo isn't exceptional, nothing else matters.
