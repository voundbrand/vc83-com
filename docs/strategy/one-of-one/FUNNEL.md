# One of One — Sales Funnel & Website Architecture

**Last updated:** 2026-02-27

---

## The Miller Framework (Marketing Made Simple), Applied

Donald Miller's funnel has five pieces: curiosity, enlightenment, commitment, the one-liner, and the sales page. We're using the structure but rewriting the playbook for an ICP that's been through every funnel on the internet and can smell a template from the first scroll.

---

## The Headline

**Private AI. You can Trust.**

This is the North Star. Five words. Every piece of copy — landing page, emails, app store, pitch decks, BNI introductions — serves this line. The period after "AI" forces a pause. Two statements that read as one promise. The capital T gives "Trust" the weight of a category name, not a casual claim.

---

## The One-Liner

Three parts. Memorizable. Repeatable. Works at a dinner, in a BNI group, on a podcast, in a DM.

**The problem:** "Every successful business owner hits a ceiling — not because they can't grow, but because nothing they've hired or bought can hold what they carry in their head."

**The solution:** "I build one AI operator around them — private, sovereign, built specifically on their business, their clients, and their judgment."

**The result:** "It's the first hire that actually compounds. C-suite leverage, live in seven days, no C-suite payroll."

**At dinner:** "You know how every business owner eventually hits a point where they're the bottleneck — not because they're bad at delegating, but because nothing holds what they know? I build private AI around them specifically. One operator that actually learns their business. It's the first thing they've ever had that compounds instead of needing more from them. Not a deck. The thing."

**Key phrases that stick:**
- "Private AI. You can Trust."
- "The first hire that actually compounds."
- "Not a deck. The thing."
- "C-suite leverage without the C-suite."

## The Landing Page Copy

### Structure (Single Page, Six Sections)

**Section 1: The Hook**

**Headline:** Private AI. You can Trust.

**Subhead:** One operator. Yours alone. Built on everything you know.

**Action line:** Talk to it. Right now. On this page.

---

**Section 2: The Problem (Three Paragraphs)**

Name the cost. Not "businesses struggle with efficiency." Specific: "You spent 45 minutes this Monday figuring out your cash position. You'll do it again next Monday. That's 39 hours a year of the most expensive person in the company doing a calculator's job."

Name the failed solutions. "You've hired VAs. You've tried ChatGPT. You've bought software that promised to change everything and added one more login to manage. The problem isn't the tools. The problem is that nothing you've tried can hold what you carry in your head — the context, the instincts, the relationship memory that makes your business *yours.*"

Name the gap. "Every tool you own holds one slice. None of them see the full picture. You do. And that's why you're still the bottleneck."

---

**Section 3: The Shift (Two Paragraphs)**

"We didn't build another tool. We built an operator — a single AI system that learns your business the way you know your business. Not as isolated functions. As one connected thing where sales affects operations affects cash flow affects your Tuesday decisions."

"It starts by listening. Within a week, it starts suggesting. Within a month, it operates in your voice. Within six months, it catches patterns you haven't noticed yet. One operator. Yours. Built on you."

---

**Section 4: The Embedded Chat**

Full-width. Prominent. Not hidden in a corner. Not a chatbot bubble.

Header above the chat: **"Don't take our word for it. Talk to it."**

Subtext: "Private AI that learns your business in seven minutes. Tell it who you are. It will find the highest-leverage workflow you should automate first. It's yours whether you work with us or not."

The chat IS the lead magnet. The chat IS the demo. The chat IS the proof.

The operator in the widget runs a specific seven-minute audit flow:

1. "What does your business do, and roughly what revenue are you at?" (Qualifies + context)
2. "How many people on your team?" (Scopes the operation)
3. "Walk me through a typical Monday morning. What's the first thing you deal with?" (Finds the pain)
4. "Of everything on your plate this week, what's the one thing you wish someone else could handle — but nobody does it the way you would?" (Finds the lever)
5. "If I could hand you back 10 hours this week, what would you spend them on?" (Opens the aspiration)

Then it delivers: "Based on what you've told me, here's the workflow that would give you the most leverage..." — specific, actionable, take-it-or-leave-it.

After the workflow delivery: "You just spent seven minutes with an operator that doesn't know you yet. Imagine what it does after six months of learning your business."

---

**Section 5: The Proof (Case Studies — Compact)**

Four proof blocks. Same format as Chapter 7 of the book:

Marcus. Painting. Stuttgart. €2.4M. 78-hour weeks → 44. Close rate 22% → 41%.
Rachel. Consulting. Dublin. €1.8M. Website conversion 1.2% → 3.8%.

No testimonial quotes. Just numbers. The reader does the math.

## The Anti-Funnel Philosophy

What this funnel does NOT do:

1. **No pop-ups.** Ever. The reader chose to come here. Respect that.
2. **No countdown timers.** This ICP spots manufactured urgency instantly.
3. **No "limited spots available."** If you have to create scarcity, your offer isn't good enough.
4. **No 47-email drip sequence.** Five emails. Quality over quantity. The audit deliverable does the work.
5. **No chatbot bubble in the corner.** The operator chat is full-width, center-page, the main event — not a support widget.
6. **No "just checking in" follow-ups.** The audit and five emails either convert or they don't. Chasing multi-millionaires is a losing play.
7. **No bait-and-switch free tier.** The free app tier works. It's not a demo. It's the product with a lower ceiling. The reader upgrades when the operator proves itself, not when a paywall blocks them.

## Voice Rules for All Funnel Copy

Same Voice Bible as the book. Load `VOICE_BIBLE.md` and `ICP_ANCHOR.md` before writing any page copy or email.

Additional funnel-specific rules:

1. **Every piece of copy should work if the reader never reads anything else.** The landing page stands alone. Each email stands alone. The audit deliverable stands alone. No "as we discussed" or "as you may have seen." Every touchpoint is self-contained.

2. **The embedded chat is the hero.** Everything on the page is either leading to the chat or supporting what the chat delivered. The page is a frame around the experience.

3. **"Start a conversation" replaces every CTA that would normally say "Book a call" or "Schedule a demo."** The language matters. A conversation is two-way. A demo is a pitch.

4. **Remington's voice appears once, at the bottom.** The page isn't about the founder. It's about the reader. But the single personal line — "I'm not an AI consultant. I'm a technologist who builds private AI." — lands because it's the only personal note on an otherwise reader-focused page.

## Chat Widget Spec Notes

### What the Embedded Operator Needs to Do

1. **Run the five-question audit flow** naturally — not as a form, as a conversation. The operator should feel like a sharp chief of staff in their first interview, not a survey bot.

2. **Deliver the workflow recommendation** at the end — specific, actionable, with enough detail that the reader could go build it themselves. This is the give-away. This is what other people charge €500–€2,000 for.

3. **Capture lead data organically** — name, email, business type, revenue range, time sinks — through the conversation, not through a form. The operator asks these as part of the natural audit flow. Email capture happens when the operator says: "I'll send you this workflow in a clean format. What's the best email?"

4. **Transition to the offer naturally** — after delivering the workflow: "You just spent seven minutes with an operator that doesn't know you yet. Imagine what it does after six months." Then the three paths appear below the chat.

5. **Connect to the app experience** — if the reader downloads the app after the chat, the operator should *remember* the audit conversation. The continuity is the proof of concept. They shouldn't have to re-explain their business.

### What the Operator Must NOT Do in the Widget

1. Never push for a sale. The workflow is the delivery. The reader decides next steps.
2. Never say "I'm just an AI" or "I'm limited in what I can do here." Confidence without disclaimers.
3. Never ask for the email before delivering value. The workflow comes first. The email capture comes after, as a service ("let me send you this in a clean format").
4. Never rush. If the reader wants to talk for fifteen minutes instead of seven, the operator engages. The seven-minute target is a minimum, not a maximum.
