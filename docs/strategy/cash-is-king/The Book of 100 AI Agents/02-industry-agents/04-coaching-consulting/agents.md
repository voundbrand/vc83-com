#### Agent #49 — The Discovery Call Navigator

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Qualifies inbound leads, prepares discovery call briefings, and optimizes the conversion pathway from inquiry to signed engagement |
| **Built On** | Closer + Coach |
| **Key Behaviors** | - Pre-qualifies prospects via structured intake forms assessing budget, timeline, problem severity, and decision authority - Prepares a one-page prospect briefing for the coach/consultant including company research, likely pain points, and recommended engagement angle - Sends post-call follow-up sequences with proposal timelines and social proof relevant to the prospect's industry |
| **Best For** | Coaches and consultants who spend 30%+ of their time on discovery calls that don't convert |
| **Hours Saved / Week** | 7 hrs |
| **Revenue Influence / Month** | €4,000 - €10,000 |
| **Tier** | Foundation |

**See It In Action**

**Before:** Katharina is a leadership coach in Frankfurt. She specializes in C-suite transitions and charges €6,500 per engagement. Last quarter, she received 12 discovery call requests — and spent over 10 hours on calls with people who were never going to buy. Two were HR managers "exploring options" with no budget approval. Three were solopreneurs who wanted free advice disguised as a "chemistry check." One ghosted after 45 minutes of deeply personal conversation. Katharina converted 2 of 12. That is a 17% conversion rate and roughly 50 minutes of unpaid labor per call. She starts dreading her own calendar. The worst part: she knows at least two serious buyers slipped through because by the time she got to them, she was exhausted and under-prepared. The feast-or-famine cycle is not just a revenue problem — it is an energy problem.

**After:** Katharina activates the Discovery Call Navigator. Every inbound inquiry now hits a structured intake form first — five questions that assess budget range, timeline, decision authority, and problem urgency. The agent scores each prospect on a 1-10 qualification matrix.

Of this month's 12 requests, the Navigator categorizes them in minutes:

> **4 qualified (score 8+):** Budget confirmed, decision-maker, active pain point. Scheduled for discovery calls with full briefing packets.

> **3 nurture-track (score 5-7):** Genuine interest but early-stage. Added to a 6-week value sequence with relevant case studies.

> **5 disqualified (score 1-4):** No budget, seeking free advice, or mismatched needs. Receive a gracious redirect email with free resources.

For each qualified call, the Navigator prepares a one-page prospect dossier. One stands out: Falk Industrietechnik just announced a restructuring in their Handelsblatt filing. The Navigator flags this:

> "Prospect: Claudia Werner, CHRO, Falk Industrietechnik. Company is mid-restructuring with 3 leadership roles in transition. Recommended angle: your Change Leadership Acceleration program. Attach the Bremer Werke case study — 92% leadership retention through their 2023 restructuring. Estimated engagement value: €18,000-€24,000."

Katharina walks into that call knowing more about Claudia's situation than Claudia expects. She converts 3 of 4 qualified calls. Total time on discovery calls this month: 3 hours instead of 10. Total conversions: 3 instead of 2. Revenue from those 3 engagements: €19,500.

**The Math:** 10 hours of unqualified calls compressed to 3 focused hours. Conversion rate jumps from 17% (2/12) to 75% (3/4 qualified). Pipeline value increases by €6,500/month with fewer hours worked.

#### Agent #55 — The Proposal Builder

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Creates customized, conversion-optimized proposals and statements of work from discovery call insights and standard templates |
| **Built On** | Closer + Copywriter + CFO |
| **Key Behaviors** | - Generates tailored proposals within 24 hours of discovery calls, using structured templates that incorporate prospect-specific pain points - Includes ROI projections, relevant case studies, and social proof matched to the prospect's industry and challenge - Tracks proposal status and triggers follow-up sequences at optimal intervals |
| **Best For** | Consultants who lose deals because proposals arrive late, look generic, or fail to connect solution to problem |
| **Hours Saved / Week** | 6 hrs |
| **Revenue Influence / Month** | €4,000 - €10,000 |
| **Tier** | Foundation |

**See It In Action**

**Before:** Marco is a management consultant in Dusseldorf. He specializes in operational excellence for mid-size manufacturers and charges €8,000-€45,000 per engagement. He just had a stellar discovery call with the HR Director of a Maschinenbau company in Wuppertal — the kind of call where everything clicks. The prospect described three urgent problems: employee retention is hemorrhaging in production (18% turnover), there is no leadership pipeline for the next generation of Meister, and absence rates hit 11% last quarter. Marco knows he can solve all three. But here is what happens next: Marco opens a blank document. He stares at it. He has client deliverables due Thursday. A workshop to prep for Friday. The proposal sits in his "weekend" pile. He finally sends it 9 days later — a solid, thoughtful 14-page document. But when he follows up on Monday, the HR Director says, "We already signed with another firm. Their proposal arrived the same week." Marco lost a €42,000 engagement because his proposal was better but slower. This has happened three times this year. He is not losing on quality. He is losing on speed.

**After:** Marco activates the Proposal Builder. His discovery call with the HR Director ends at 11:00 AM on a Tuesday. He spends 10 minutes after the call dictating his notes into the agent: key pain points, budget signals, decision timeline, and the personal priorities the HR Director mentioned.

By 1:00 PM, the Proposal Builder delivers a draft. Not a template with blanks filled in — a 12-page proposal that reads like Marco wrote it over a weekend:

> **Opening:** "In our conversation this morning, you described three challenges that are costing Hartmann Maschinenbau an estimated €640K annually in turnover-related costs alone..."

> **Solution architecture:** A 6-month "People Performance Transformation" program structured in 3 phases — Diagnostic (weeks 1-4), Design (weeks 5-12), and Embed (weeks 13-24) — with milestone-based pricing tied to measurable outcomes.

> **Social proof:** The Brinkmann Metallwerke case study, automatically selected because it matches on industry, company size, and problem profile. That engagement reduced turnover from 16% to 7% in 8 months.

> **ROI projection:** "Based on your current turnover rate of 18% across 340 production employees, reducing to the industry benchmark of 9% represents approximately €180K in annual savings from reduced recruitment, onboarding, and productivity loss."

Marco reviews it, adjusts two paragraphs, and sends it at 3:00 PM — four hours after the call. The HR Director replies at 4:30 PM: "This is exactly what we discussed. Can we schedule the kick-off?"

The competitor's proposal arrives 9 days later. By then, Marco has already signed the €42,000 engagement and scheduled the diagnostic phase.

**The Math:** Proposal turnaround drops from 9 days to 4 hours. Win rate on competitive deals jumps from 30% to 65%. At Marco's average engagement size of €28K, that translates to roughly €11,200/month in recovered revenue that was previously lost to speed alone.

#### Agent #62 — The Revenue Diversifier

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Identifies and develops new revenue streams from existing expertise, audience, and intellectual property |
| **Built On** | Strategist + CFO + Closer |
| **Key Behaviors** | - Maps the consultant's IP assets and identifies productization opportunities: courses, templates, assessments, licensing - Models revenue scenarios for new offerings including development costs, pricing, and breakeven timelines - Designs launch strategies for new revenue streams that leverage the existing audience without cannibalizing core offerings |
| **Best For** | Established consultants at revenue plateaus who need to break through the billable-hours ceiling |
| **Hours Saved / Week** | 5 hrs |
| **Revenue Influence / Month** | €3,500 - €8,000 |
| **Tier** | Sovereign |

**See It In Action**

**Before:** Nadine is a change management consultant based in Cologne. She has 15 years of experience, a reputation that fills her calendar, and a revenue number that has not moved in three years: €280K. Every single euro of that requires her physical presence — in workshops, in boardrooms, on calls. She works 52 billable hours a week. She turned down two new clients last month because she literally has no hours left to sell. Her accountant tells her to raise rates. She already did — twice. Her clients pay €1,800/day and that is near the ceiling for her market segment. Nadine has intellectual property that other consultants would pay for: a proprietary change readiness assessment she developed over a decade, workshop frameworks tested across 60+ organizations, and enough case study material to fill a book. But all of it lives in her head, her slide decks, and her filing cabinet. She knows she needs to "productize" but every time she sits down to figure out how, a client calls and the urgent eats the important. She is successful and stuck. The trap of trading hours for money looks like a corner office until you realize it is still a trap.

**After:** Nadine activates the Revenue Diversifier. The agent starts by auditing her existing IP — every framework, assessment, workshop module, and case study she has created over 15 years. It maps them against four revenue diversification models and presents a prioritized plan:

> **Revenue Stream 1 — License the Change Readiness Assessment (Quick Win)**
> "Your CRA tool is already used informally by 3 former colleagues. Formalize it: €200/license for independent consultants, €1,200/year for firms. Target market: 400+ change management consultants in DACH. Conservative estimate: 80 licenses in year one = €16,000. Development cost: €2,400 (packaging, documentation, scoring guide). Breakeven: month 2."

> **Revenue Stream 2 — "Leading Change" Online Cohort (Medium Lift)**
> "Your 2-day workshop content converts into a 6-week online cohort program. €2,400 per participant, 15 per cohort, 4 cohorts per year. You facilitate 90 minutes per week — the rest is peer learning and async content. Annual revenue: €144,000. Development cost: €8,500 (video production, platform setup, workbook design). Breakeven: first cohort."

> **Revenue Stream 3 — Change Leader Certification (Strategic Play)**
> "Train internal change champions at client organizations. 3-day intensive + 8-week practicum + certification exam. €6,800 per participant. This creates embedded advocates who generate repeat engagements for your 1:1 practice. 20 participants per year = €136,000. Development cost: €14,000. Breakeven: 3 participants."

> **Revenue Stream 4 — Book Proposal (Authority Builder)**
> "Your 60+ client case studies are a book. Working title: 'The Change Paradox: Why 70% of Transformations Fail and What the Other 30% Know.' This is not a revenue stream — it is an authority multiplier that raises rates on everything else by 15-25%. Development timeline: 9 months. Agent handles research compilation, chapter structure, and publisher pitch."

The Revenue Diversifier builds a phased launch timeline: Stream 1 in months 1-2, Stream 2 in months 3-5, Stream 3 in months 6-9, Stream 4 running in parallel. It models the combined scenario: by month 18, Nadine's new revenue streams generate €142,000 annually. Her total revenue reaches €422K — a 50% increase — and the new streams require roughly 8 hours per week instead of the 52 her consulting practice demands.

For the first time in three years, Nadine's revenue is growing. And for the first time in her career, not every euro requires her to be in the room.

**The Math:** From €280K (100% time-dependent) to €422K (with €142K from scalable streams) in 18 months. That is a 50% revenue increase requiring 15% more hours — flipping the hours-to-revenue ratio from 1:1 to exponential.
