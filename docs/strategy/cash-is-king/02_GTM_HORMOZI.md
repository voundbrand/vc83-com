## The Framework

This GTM follows Hormozi's $100M Leads framework. There are only four ways to get leads:

```
1. Warm Outreach    — reach out to people who know you
2. Cold Outreach    — reach out to people who don't
3. Free Content     — attract people to you
4. Paid Ads         — amplify what works
```

Plus: every closed client feeds the **referral engine** — your 5th compounding channel.

**The Rule of 100:** Every day, do 100 of something. 100 outreach messages. 100 minutes of content. Or €100 in ads. No days off from lead gen.

## B. The Core Four + Referral Engine

### B.1 Warm Outreach (40% of Year 1 clients)

**Hormozi:** *"The simplest way to get leads is to reach out to people who already know you."*

This is day one, dollar zero lead gen. Your phone, your WhatsApp contacts, your LinkedIn connections, your friends, your former colleagues, people you've met at events. Everyone you know hears about this.

#### The List

Before you send a single message, build the list:

| Source | Expected Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Phone contacts (personal WhatsApp) | 100-300 | Everyone you've ever messaged |
| LinkedIn connections | 500+ | Filter for business owners, DACH |
| Former colleagues / clients | 20-50 | People who know your work |
| Friends and family (who own businesses or know people who do) | 30-50 | Ask for intros, not sales |
| Local Pasewalk / MV business owners you know by name | 10-30 | In-person coffee invites |
| **Total warm list** | **300-500+ people** | |

#### The Message (Warm — people who know you)

Not a pitch. A conversation starter:

> **For people you know well:**
> "Hey [Name] — I built something I'm really proud of. It's a personal AI agent that's born from a 15-minute conversation about your business. After that it lives on WhatsApp, follows up with leads, answers questions — 24/7. Can I show you in 15 minutes? No pitch, I just want your honest reaction."

> **For people you know but not closely:**
> "Hey [Name] — long time. Quick question: do you know any business owners who are overwhelmed with admin, follow-ups, or lead management? I built an AI system that handles that — it's genuinely impressive. If you or anyone you know would be curious, I'd love to show it."

> **For friends / family (not business owners):**
> "Hey [Name] — I'm launching something and need your help. Do you know anyone who runs a business and would be interested in having an AI that knows their business inside out? I'll make it worth your while if you intro me."

#### The Math (Hormozi-style)

| Stage | Number | Conversion |
|---|---|---|
| Warm contacts messaged (Month 1-3) | 300 | — |
| Replies / conversations started | 150 | 50% (these people know you) |
| "Who do you know?" referral intros received | 30-50 | 1 in 3-5 knows someone |
| Discovery calls booked (you + referred) | 40-50 | ~80% of interested |
| Live birthing demos | 25-30 | ~60% of calls |
| Proposals sent | 15-20 | ~65% of demos |
| **Closed deals (Month 1-6)** | **7-8** | **~45% of proposals** |

**Expected from warm outreach: 7-8 clients in Year 1** — this is your primary engine for the first 6 months.

#### Why Warm Works for High-Ticket

Hormozi: *"Trust is already established. The close rate is highest here. Start here."*

- They already trust you → shorter sales cycle
- They'll give honest feedback → iterate faster
- They'll refer others → the referral engine starts
- Foundation clients come from here (low risk for them, fast close for you)
- Dream Team clients come from here too (business owners who see the demo and go "I need the full thing")

#### The "Who Do You Know?" Question

This is the single most important question in warm outreach. After every conversation — whether they buy or not — ask:

> "This clearly isn't for everyone, but who do you know that's a business owner drowning in admin or follow-ups? I'll give them a free demo, and if they end up working with me, I'll [take you to dinner / send you a bottle of wine / give you a month free]."

Hormozi calls this the **three-way introduction via text**: "Hey [Friend], meet [You] — he built something wild, you should see it. [You], meet [Friend], she runs [Business]."

### B.2 Cold Outreach (25% of Year 1 clients)

**Hormozi:** *"Cold outreach is contacting strangers directly. Since trust doesn't exist, success depends on volume, a compelling offer, and repeated contact."*

Once warm outreach is rolling (Month 2+), layer in cold.

#### Target ICPs for Cold Outreach

| ICP | Why | Where to Find Them |
|---|---|---|
| **Lawyers / Anwaltskanzleien** (DACH) | Privacy-obsessed, high-value per client, need 24/7 intake → Sovereign tier | LinkedIn, Anwaltverzeichnis, IHK |
| **Financial advisors / Finanzberater** | Compliance-heavy, need lead nurturing, client comms → Dream Team | LinkedIn, BaFin registry, industry events |
| **Coaches / Berater** | Already sell transformation, understand the value of AI leverage → Foundation/Dream Team | Instagram, LinkedIn, podcast guesting |
| **Agency owners** (marketing, HR, recruitment) | Need to scale delivery without hiring, margin pressure → Dream Team | LinkedIn, Clutch.co, agency directories |
| **Medical practices / Arztpraxen** | Patient communication, appointment management, privacy → Sovereign | LinkedIn, Ärzteblatt, KV directories |
| **Handwerk / trade business owners** (€1M+) | Admin overload, lead management, quote follow-up → Foundation/Dream Team | IHK Handwerksverzeichnis, local networks |

#### The Cold DM Framework (LinkedIn)

**Step 1 — Research (2 min per prospect):**
Look at their profile, company, recent posts. Find one specific thing to reference.

**Step 2 — The Opening DM (no pitch):**

> "[Name], saw your post about [topic] — really resonated. Quick question: how much time does your team spend on follow-ups and client communication per week? Asking because I've been working on something that handles that entirely with AI."

**Step 3 — The Value Drop (after they reply):**

> "Here's the short version: I build AI agents that are born from a 15-minute conversation about a business. After that, they handle WhatsApp, email, lead follow-up, client questions — 24/7. My last client was a [similar business] and they saved [specific hours/money]. Worth 15 min of your time to see it live?"

**Step 4 — The Birthing Demo Offer:**

> "I'll birth your AI live — takes 15 minutes, no cost, no obligation. If it's impressive, we talk next steps. If not, you got a free AI that knows your business. Fair?"

Create a digital guide: **"100 AI Agents for Your Business — What They Do, How They Work, and Which Ones You Need"**

| Attribute | Details |

### B.4 Paid Ads (5% of Year 1 clients — seeds Year 2)

**Hormozi:** *"Paid ads are one-to-many cold outreach. Only spend money once you know what converts organically."*

**Month 1-6:** Zero ad spend. Prove the offer works through warm + cold.
**Month 7+:** Test small budgets on what's already working.

#### When to Start Paid Ads

Only after you can answer YES to all three:
1. ✅ You've closed 5+ clients and know which pitch converts
2. ✅ You have 2+ case studies with real numbers
3. ✅ Your birthing demo has been proven to close in person and on Zoom

#### What to Advertise

| Ad Type | Platform | Budget | CTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lead magnet ("Book of 100 Agents") | LinkedIn Ads | €500/month test | Download → email sequence → demo booking |
| Birthing demo video (15 min condensed to 2 min) | YouTube pre-roll | €300/month test | "Watch your AI come alive — book a free demo" |
| Retargeting (website visitors) | Meta/LinkedIn | €200/month test | "Ready to meet your AI? Book 15 min." |

#### Paid Ads Math (When Running)

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Cost per lead (download) | €5-15 |
| Cost per discovery call | €50-100 |
| Cost per closed client | €500-1,500 |
| Client LTV Year 1 (Foundation) | €10,988 |
| **ROAS** | **7-22x** |

**Expected from paid ads in Year 1: 1-2 clients** (testing only). **Year 2: major scale channel.**

### B.5 The Referral Engine (10% of Year 1 — 30%+ by Year 2)

**Hormozi:** *"Every customer should become a lead getter. Make it easy, make it instant, make it rewarding."*

This is the compounding channel. Every client you close feeds the next one.

#### The Three-Way Introduction (Immediately After Close)

The moment a client pays — before delivery even starts — ask:

> "I'm thrilled to work together. Quick question: who's the first person you'd want to tell about this? A friend, a business contact, someone in a similar situation? I'll give them a free birthing demo, and if they come on board, I'll give you [a month free / a custom agent / a strategy session]."

**Don't ask for names and numbers.** Ask them to make a three-way text intro right then:

> "[Client], can you text them right now? Just something like: 'Hey [Friend], I just signed up for something wild — an AI that knows my business. You should see it. Here's [You]'s number.'"

This is warm outreach powered by social proof. The referred person already knows the client bought. The trust transfer is instant.

#### The Referral Incentive Structure

| Tier Client Is On | Referral Reward | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Foundation (€5K) | 1 free month (€499 value) | Low friction, easy to give |
| Dream Team (€35K) | 1 custom agent added to their setup (€2K value) | They want more agents anyway |
| Sovereign (€135K+) | Free quarterly on-site optimization (€3K value) OR monthly fee waived for 1 month | High-touch, deepens relationship |

#### The Referral Math

| Metric | Conservative | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Clients who refer at least 1 person | 40% | 60% |
| Referred prospects who take a demo | 60% | 75% |
| Referred demos who close | 40% | 50% |
| **Referral multiplier per client** | 0.10 new clients | 0.23 new clients |

With 18 clients in Year 1:
- Conservative: 18 × 0.10 = ~2 referred clients
- Target: 18 × 0.23 = ~4 referred clients

**Expected from referrals in Year 1: 2-4 clients**

**But the compound effect is massive:**
- Year 1: 18 clients → 2-4 referrals
- Year 2: 30+ clients → 7-12 referrals (each referral also refers)
- Year 3: 50+ clients → 15-25 referrals
- By Year 3, referrals should be your #1 channel

#### Making Every Client a Case Study

Every client is a potential:
- **Testimonial** (text quote for website/LinkedIn)
- **Case study** (full story with numbers)
- **Video testimonial** (30 seconds, phone-shot is fine)
- **Co-speaking opportunity** ("Come to this event with me and tell people what the AI did for your business")
- **Referral source** (the three-way intro above)

Ask for all five at the 30-day check-in, when they're happiest.

## D. The Sales Process

### D.1 From First Contact to Close

```
First Contact (warm intro, cold DM reply, inbound from content, referral)
        ↓ within 24 hours
Discovery Call (30 min — understand their business, pain, budget)
        ↓ 3-7 days
Live Birthing Demo (15 min — their AI comes alive, they feel it)
        ↓ 1-3 days
Proposal (tier recommendation based on needs + budget)
        ↓ 3-7 days
Decision Call (handle objections, close or negotiate)
        ↓ 1-7 days
Payment (50% upfront, 50% on delivery — OR 100% upfront for Foundation)
        ↓ 1-4 weeks
Delivery + Onboarding
        ↓ 30 days after delivery
Referral Ask + Case Study Ask + Testimonial Ask
```

**Average sales cycle:**
- Foundation: 1-2 weeks
- Dream Team: 2-4 weeks
- Sovereign: 4-8 weeks

### D.2 Pricing Psychology

| Tactic | How to Use It |
|---|---|
| **Always present Sovereign first** | Anchor high. Even if they buy Foundation, they know there's more. "Most of my clients at your level go with the €35K package, but let me show you the full range." |
| **Show the alternative cost** | "A custom AI project from an agency runs €100-300K. The equivalent in-house hire costs €73-104K/year in Germany." |
| **The 15-minute birthing demo** | This IS the close. Nobody competes with watching their AI come alive. The emotional attachment is immediate. |
| **"The AI remembers"** | Soul depth = switching cost. "In 3 months, leaving this AI feels like firing a team member who knows everything about your business." |
| **Monthly fee as "health insurance"** | "The €499/month is your AI's ongoing care — updates, monitoring, new models, and someone to call when you need changes." |
| **Payment terms (Sovereign)** | 50% upfront (€67,500). That deposit funds the hardware. The other 50% on delivery. Zero risk to you. |
| **"Give away the secrets"** | Share the Book of 100 Agents freely. Give a free birthing. Be generous. The sale happens because they realize they can't implement this themselves. |

### D.3 Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "That's expensive" | "What does your team cost per year? This replaces 3-5 roles, works 24/7, never calls in sick. One captured lead at your average deal size pays for the whole thing." |
| "Can't I just use ChatGPT?" | "ChatGPT forgets you every session. This AI has 7 layers of memory that make it permanently yours. Watch — let me birth one right now. You'll feel the difference in 15 minutes." |
| "Why local hardware?" | "Your client data is your most valuable asset. With Sovereign, nothing ever leaves your building. No cloud. No third parties. No breach risk. Just your AI, on your desk, answering your clients." |
| "I need to think about it" | "Completely fair. Let me leave the AI we just birthed running for 7 days — talk to it, see what it does. If it doesn't impress you, no hard feelings." |
| "My IT person can build this" | "They could — in 6-12 months, for €100K+ in salary and infrastructure. You'll have it running on your desk in 2 weeks." |
| "We're not tech-savvy" | "Perfect. You just had a conversation with me. Your AI was born. That's the entire setup. If you can use WhatsApp, you can use this." |
| "I don't have that budget right now" | "That's okay. The Foundation package is €5K — less than a part-time VA for a month. Want to start there and upgrade when you see the results?" |

## K. Optional Accelerator: BNI Chapter Director (Pasewalk)

BNI is not required for this strategy to work. But if you choose to invest the time, it's a high-ROI supplementary channel.

### The Opportunity

There's no BNI chapter in Pasewalk. Starting one as Chapter Director:
- Positions you as the business leader in the region
- Gives you the exclusive AI / technology seat (no competitors)
- Connects you to the MV Regionaldirektor → access to Rostock, Greifswald, Neubrandenburg chapters
- BNI members self-select for your ICP: service business owners, €500K-5M revenue, value trust and referrals

### The Math

| Investment | Amount |
|---|---|
| Membership (likely waived as director) | €0-1,420/year |
| One-time fee | €390 |
| Time | 5-6 hours/week = ~275 hours/year |

| Return (conservative) | Amount |
|---|---|
| Expected clients from BNI referrals | 3-5/year |
| Revenue from those clients | €200K-400K |
| **ROI on time** | **€730-1,450/hour** |

### When to Add BNI

| Condition | Add BNI? |
|---|---|
| Warm + cold outreach filling your pipeline | Wait — you're at capacity |
| Outreach slowing, need more local leads | Yes — BNI fills the DACH SMB pipeline |
| You want long-term network compounding | Yes — BNI compounds over 2-3 years |
| You want the Regionaldirektor relationship | Yes — strategic play beyond lead gen |
| You're already doing 40 hrs/week and maxed | No — BNI adds 5.5 hrs you can't spare |

### The Up-Chain Play

If you do BNI, the real value isn't the local chapter. It's the network effect:

```
You (Chapter Director, Pasewalk)
    → solid relationship with Regionaldirektor MV
    → cross-chapter visits (Rostock, Greifswald, Neubrandenburg)
    → speaking at regional BNI events
    → known across MV as "the AI guy"
    → 100+ second-degree referral sources
```

BNI members have networks of 50-200+ contacts each. A 30-member chapter = 1,500-6,000 second-degree connections. As director, you're the hub.

**But the strategy works without it.** Hormozi built a $250M portfolio without BNI. The Core Four are the engine. BNI is a turbocharger you bolt on when the engine is running.
