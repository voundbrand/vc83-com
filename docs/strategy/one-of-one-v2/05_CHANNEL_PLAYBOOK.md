## Channel Mix Overview

The online funnel is now primary. Offline channels (BNI, IHK, events) are force multipliers that drive traffic to the funnel and create the warm context that makes the funnel convert.

| Channel | Role | % of Year 1 Clients | Primary Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Online funnel (landing page)** | Primary inbound engine | 30% | Diagnostic + demo booking |
| **BNI** | Network asset + referral engine | 30% | Warm intros → landing page or direct demo |
| **Direct outreach (LinkedIn + warm network)** | Rule of 100 daily discipline | 20% | DMs → landing page or call booking |
| **Live demos & speaking** | Trust accelerator | 10% | Events → immediate demo → beta code |
| **Strategic partnerships** | Leverage other people's networks | 10% | Referral commission agreements |

## Channel 1: Online Funnel (Primary)

### The Landing Page

The one-of-one landing page is the center of the GTM. Every other channel points here.

**Two CTAs, always visible:**

1. **"Start Your Diagnostic"** — Opens Samantha chat. 7-minute AI audit.
2. **"Book a Live Demo"** — Links to Remington's calendar. 30-minute founder-led demo.

**Traffic sources:**
- LinkedIn posts and DMs (link in every touchpoint)
- BNI members sharing the link with their networks
- QR code on business cards, event materials
- Google organic (SEO plays develop over months)
- Referral links from existing clients

### The Conversion Path

```
Landing page visit
    ├── AI Diagnostic (Samantha)
    │   → Completes audit → sees handoff CTAs
    │   → Enters platform or books call
    │   → 7-day trial → Day 7 close call
    │
    └── Book a Live Demo
        → Founder-led 30-min demo
        → Beta code → 7-day trial
        → Day 7 close call
```

### What Makes This Channel Work

- Available 24/7 (the AI never sleeps)
- Zero marginal cost per visitor (~€0.15–0.30 for diagnostic)
- The diagnostic is itself the proof of product
- Full attribution tracking from first touch to close
- Scales without scaling Remington's time

## Channel 2: BNI

BNI strategy carries forward from cash-is-king with one key update: **every BNI touchpoint now drives to the landing page or a direct demo, not a beta code hand-off.**

### The BNI Loop

```
BNI Chapter Meeting
    → 60-second pitch (Meta Glasses demo or verbal)
    → Networking break: hand out cards with QR → landing page
    → 1:1 coffees midweek: "Go to the website, talk to Samantha, tell me what you think"
    → Their network: "You should talk to Remington" → landing page link
    → Warm intro → direct demo or diagnostic
```

### BNI Roles

| Role | Status | Strategic Value |
|---|---|---|
| **Chapter Director, Pasewalk** | Target | Maximum visibility, referral priority, dues likely waived |
| **"AI / Technology" seat** | Lock it | Category exclusivity — no competitors in the chapter |
| **Cross-chapter visitor** | Active | Present at Greifswald, Neubrandenburg, Rostock, Schwerin chapters |
| **Regionaldirektor MV relationship** | Build | Opens access to every chapter in the region |

### BNI Pitch Updates

The BNI pitch still uses the Meta Glasses demo (Doc 06 in cash-is-king). But the follow-up has changed:

| Old Follow-Up | New Follow-Up |
|---|---|
| "Here's a beta code" | "Go to sevenlayers.io — talk to my AI. Tell me what you think." |
| "7-day free trial" | "The diagnostic is free and takes 7 minutes. If you like what it tells you, I'll set up a call." |
| "WhatsApp me for a code" | "Book a demo at sevenlayers.io/demo — or just start chatting with Samantha on the homepage." |

Everything routes to the landing page or the demo booking. The funnel does the qualification work.

### BNI Time Investment

| Activity | Hours/Week |
|---|---|
| Chapter meeting | 2.0 |
| Post-meeting follow-ups | 0.5 |
| 1:1 coffees (2 per week) | 2.0 |
| Admin (visitor invitations, referral tracking, chapter growth) | 1.0 |
| **Total** | **5.5 hours/week** |

### BNI ROI Target

| Investment | Amount |
|---|---|
| Membership | €1,420/year (or €0 if waived as director) |
| Time | ~275 hours/year |
| **Return (conservative)** | **5–7 clients/year = €35K–€1.3M in setup revenue** |

## Channel 3: Direct Outreach (Rule of 100)

### LinkedIn

| Activity | Daily | Weekly |
|---|---|---|
| Send personalized DMs to ICP prospects | 15–20 | 75–100 |
| Comment on target prospects' posts | 10–15 | 50–75 |
| Publish original content (posts, articles) | — | 3–5 posts |
| Share client results / case studies | — | 1/week |

**DM Template (adapt, never copy-paste):**

> "Hey [Name], saw your post about [topic]. I built something that might interest you — an AI operator that handles [relevant task] for businesses like yours. Takes 7 minutes to see it in action: [landing page link]. No pitch, just talk to the AI and see what it recommends."

**Key principle:** Value first. Link to diagnostic. Let the AI do the qualifying. Only pitch on a call after they've experienced the product.

### Warm Network

| Activity | Monthly |
|---|---|
| WhatsApp/Signal messages to personal contacts | 30–50 |
| Local business owner coffees (Pasewalk/MV) | 8–12 |
| Event contact follow-ups | As they come |

**Warm Network Script:**

> "Hey [Name], I built something new — an AI that's born from a conversation about your business. 7 minutes on our website and it'll tell you exactly where to start. [link] Would love your honest feedback."

### Events

| Venue | Type | Frequency | Leads per Event |
|---|---|---|---|
| BNI chapter presentations (own + cross-chapter) | 10-min presentation with Meta Glasses | 2/month | 2–5 |
| IHK events (Industrie- und Handelskammer) | 30-min talk or booth | 1/quarter | 5–10 |

| Podcast guesting (DE business podcasts) | Interview + demo offer | 1–2/month | 3–5 |
| Vistage / EO groups | Invited presentation | As invited | 3–8 high-quality leads |

### The Demo Flow (Any Format)

```
1. "Let me show you something. 7 minutes."
2. Run a LIVE diagnostic — or use the Meta Glasses for maximum theater
3. The AI analyzes their business and delivers a recommendation
4. "Your operator just showed you what it can do. Want to keep it?"
5. Direct to landing page for diagnostic, or book follow-up call
```

### Meta Glasses Demo (Unchanged from Cash-Is-King Doc 06)

The BNI pitch playbook remains the tactical guide for the Glasses demo. The choreography, failover scripts, and preparation checklists are all current. The only change is the follow-up: route to the landing page or demo booking, not a beta code.

## Channel 5: Strategic Partnerships

### Partner Types

| Partner | How Seven Layers Fits | Deal Structure |
|---|---|---|
| **Marketing agencies** | "Your clients need an AI to handle the leads you generate" | 20% referral commission |
| **IT consultants / MSPs** | "Add AI operators to your service stack" | 15% referral + co-delivery option |
| **Business coaches** | "Give your clients an operator that implements what you teach" | 20% referral |
| **Steuerberater / Tax advisors** | "An AI that handles your clients' reporting and follow-up" | 15% referral |

### How to Find Them

BNI IS the partner pool. The marketing agency member, the IT consultant, the business coach — they all serve the same customers we do. Cross-referral is the native BNI motion.

### Partner Activation

1. Identify potential partner in BNI chapter
2. 1:1 coffee: show them the diagnostic ("Talk to Samantha, see what she says about your business")
3. If impressed: "Your clients would love this. Want to try it? If it works, I'll give you 20% of every client you refer."
4. Give them a custom referral link (attribution tracking built in)
5. Monthly check-in: review referrals, share results, keep the relationship warm

**Target:** 2–3 active referral partners by month 6, generating 1–2 clients/year each.

## Channel 6: Content (Credibility Layer, Not Revenue Engine)

Content is NOT the revenue engine in Year 1. It's the trust accelerator. When someone Googles you after a BNI intro or a LinkedIn DM, they should find enough to believe you're real.

| Content Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn posts | 3–5/week | Thought leadership, build-in-public |
| YouTube (short demos) | 1/month | Demo clips, customer stories |
| SEO articles | 1–2/month | Long-tail: "Personal AI for [industry]" |
| Case studies | 1 per client (with permission) | Social proof for sales conversations |

**Year 2 pivot:** Once high-ticket revenue funds a content hire, flip the content engine on. At that point, consider adding self-serve tiers (the old €49–399/mo model) as the mass-market play funded by high-ticket profits.
