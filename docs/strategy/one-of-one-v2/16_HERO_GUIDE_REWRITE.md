## Rewritten Copy — Section by Section

### Section 1: The Hook

**StoryBrand role:** Introduce the Hero. State the philosophical problem. Make them feel seen.

**Sinek method:** Start with WHY. No product. No platform. A belief.

**Fisher rules:** Short sentences. No hedging. No "we believe that perhaps..."

---

**Headline:**

> You built this business. You shouldn't have to run every piece of it.

**Subheadline:**

> One right hand. Yours alone. Built on everything you know.

**Action line:**

> Talk to it. Right now. On this page.

**Parallel paths note:**

> Two paths: run the 7-minute AI diagnostic, or book a 30-minute founder-led demo with Remington.

---

**Why this works:**

The old headline — "Private AI. You can Trust." — leads with product and asks for trust before earning it. The new headline leads with the hero. It says: *you* are the protagonist. *You* built something. And here's a belief we share — you shouldn't have to do everything alone.

"Right hand" replaces "operator" and carries trust, singularity, and enhancement in two words. It doesn't promise a tool. It promises leverage.

---

### Section 2: The Problem

**StoryBrand role:** Name the external, internal, and philosophical problem. The hero must see themselves.

**Fisher rules:** Short paragraphs. One idea per sentence. Real numbers. No adjectives.

---

**Paragraph 1 — The External Problem (the math):**

> You spent 45 minutes this Monday figuring out your cash position. You'll do it again next Monday. That's 39 hours a year. The most expensive person in the company, doing a calculator's job.

**Paragraph 2 — The Internal Problem (the frustration):**

> You've hired VAs. You've tried ChatGPT. You've bought software that promised to change everything and added one more login to manage. The problem isn't the tools. The problem is that nothing you've tried can hold what you carry in your head — the context, the instincts, the relationships that make your business *yours.*

**Paragraph 3 — The Philosophical Problem (the belief):**

> You are the most important person in this business. Every tool you own holds one slice. None of them see the full picture. You do. And that's why you're still the bottleneck.

---

**What changed from current:**

- Paragraph 1: Unchanged. It's already strong — real numbers, no fluff.
- Paragraph 2: Minor edits. "Relationship memory" becomes "relationships" — simpler. Emphasis on *yours* stays.
- Paragraph 3: Adds "You are the most important person in this business." This is the philosophical stake. It reframes the bottleneck from a negative (you're stuck) to a positive (you're indispensable — and that's the problem).

### Section 3: The Shift — Meet the Guide

**StoryBrand role:** The Guide enters. Empathy first, then authority. The Guide does NOT position themselves as the hero. The Guide has been where the hero is and found a way through.

**Sinek method:** Why before How before What.

**Fisher rules:** No "we believe that maybe..." — state it plainly.

---

**Paragraph 1 — The Guide's belief (WHY):**

> We believe business owners should do the thing they set out to do. Not the rest.

> So we built something different. Not another tool. Not another login. A right hand — a single AI that learns your business the way you know your business. Not as isolated functions. As one connected thing where sales affects operations affects cash flow affects your Tuesday decisions.

**Paragraph 2 — How it works + the promise (HOW + WHAT):**

> It starts by listening. Within a week, it starts suggesting. Within a month, it works in your voice. Within six months, it catches patterns you haven't noticed yet. One right hand. Yours. Built on you.

---

**What changed from current:**

- Opens with the Sinek "I believe" line adapted from BNI pitch Section D: "Business owners should do the thing they set out to do. Not the rest."
- "We didn't build another tool. We built an operator" becomes "Not another tool. Not another login. A right hand." Shorter. Fisher cadence. And "not another login" comes straight from Section D — it's tested language.
- "It operates in your voice" becomes "it works in your voice." Removes the word "operate" entirely.
- Everything else stays. The progression (listening → suggesting → working in your voice → catching patterns) is already excellent.

## Tone Checklist

Before finalizing any copy, run it through this filter:

| Rule | Source | Test |
|---|---|---|
| No compound sentences | Fisher | If a sentence has "and" or a comma joining two independent clauses, split it. |
| No hedging | Fisher | Zero "maybe," "perhaps," "we think," "we believe that maybe." State it. |
| Belief before product | Sinek | Does the page open with WHY before WHAT? Does the reader know what you believe before they know what you sell? |
| Hero is the customer | Miller | Is the business owner the protagonist in every sentence? Is Seven Layers always the guide, never the hero? |
| Guide shows empathy first | Miller | Does the Guide demonstrate "I've been where you are" before "here's what I built"? |
| The plan has 3 steps | Miller | Are there exactly three clear steps? Can the hero recite them from memory? |
| Stakes are named | Miller | Does the page say what happens if the hero does nothing? |
| Success is painted | Miller | Does the page show what life looks like after the transformation? |
| Numbers, not adjectives | Fisher / Section D | "14 inquiries answered. 6 appointments booked. 0 hours spent." Never "dramatically improved response times." |
| Enhancement, not replacement | Core positioning | Does every mention of AI enhance the owner? Does it ever suggest replacing a team member? |
| Land the plane | Fisher | Does every section end cleanly? No trailing "so yeah" or "let us know what you think." |

2. **The core term.** "Operator" → "Right hand" across 18 content strings.

## Summary

The current landing page has strong bones. The chat surface is world-class. The proof section is real. The pricing is clear. The privacy section earns trust.

What's broken is the opening energy and the core word.

"Operator" asks the prospect to learn a new vocabulary before they've decided to care. "Right hand" meets them where they already are — in the language they use when they describe what they wish they had.

The StoryBrand reframe is simple: make the business owner the hero of the page, not Seven Layers. Every sentence should make them feel seen, understood, and then — clearly, simply — offered a way forward.

The Sinek frame is even simpler: lead with what you believe. "Business owners should do the thing they set out to do. Not the rest." If they share that belief, they'll read the rest of the page. If they don't, they were never your customer.

The Fisher frame is the discipline: say it in fewer words. End the sentence. Move on.

One right hand. Built on you.
