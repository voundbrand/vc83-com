## Section 7: Objection Handling

**"Why not a discovery call?"**

> You've sat through enough discovery calls to know how they work. Fifteen minutes of "tell me about your business," then thirty minutes of pitch. We don't do that. The AI you just talked to is the discovery. It asked you five questions and identified your first agent. That's more value than most consultants provide in a paid engagement. If you want to work together, start a conversation. If you don't, keep the results. They're yours.

**"Why agents instead of more software?"**

> Every tool you add is another thing that doesn't know what the other things know. You end up being the integration layer — the human middleware between twelve systems that can't talk to each other. An agent doesn't sit next to your tools. It does the work your tools can't. It doesn't need you to check a dashboard. It follows up, schedules, answers, and escalates. You review the results.

## What Changed from Document 16

1. **Core term.** "Right hand" → "Agent(s)." Concrete, modular, no interpretation needed.
2. **Headline energy.** From pain ("you shouldn't have to run every piece of it") to competitive edge ("five outperform thirty").
3. **Problem framing.** From "you're the bottleneck" to "the winners are delegating to agents."
4. **Product positioning.** From one monolithic AI to modular agents, one at a time. Lower risk. Clearer value per unit.
5. **Objection handling.** "Why one right hand" → "Why agents instead of more software." Different objection, better answer.
6. **Full circle.** Page opens and closes with the same line: "Knock down your workload. One agent at a time."
7. **Hardware.** Local deployment expanded from Apple-only to Apple Silicon, NVIDIA, AMD.
