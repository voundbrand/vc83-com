### Risk 1: Hallo Kira Ships RA-MICRO Integration + Gets Traction

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Probability** | HIGH (they already list "RA-MICRO coming soon") |
| **Impact** | MEDIUM-HIGH (becomes default for RA-MICRO-using Kanzleien) |
| **Timeline** | 3-6 months |
| **Kill shot?** | Not alone. RA-MICRO is non-exclusive (multiple partners per category). But combined with their lower price, this makes them the path of least resistance. |

**Mitigation:**
- Don't race Kira to RA-MICRO. Win on outcome depth (qualification, booking, dashboard).
- Build RA-MICRO CSV export early (Month 3) as a bridge — "we generate RA-MICRO-importable files."
- Position as complementary, not competing: "Kira answers phones. We run your Mandatsannahme."
- If Kira wins RA-MICRO partnership, pivot our moat to practice area templates + booking depth.

**Monitoring:** Check hallokira.de monthly for RA-MICRO integration launch. Check RA-MICRO Kooperationspartner page for new listings.

### Risk 4: RA-MICRO Builds Own AI Phone

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Probability** | MEDIUM (they could), LOW in near-term (they haven't) |
| **Impact** | CRITICAL (70K workplaces get AI phone built-in) |
| **Timeline** | 2-3 years |
| **Kill shot?** | If they build it well, yes. But legacy software companies building AI is historically slow and bad. |

**Mitigation:**
- **Why they haven't built it yet:** Voice AI is not their competency. Their DNA is practice management software. Building multi-agent voice systems is a different engineering discipline.
- **More likely:** They partner with or acquire someone. This could be us, Hallo Kira, or a generic voice AI vendor.
- **Best case:** We become the partner they choose. Start the conversation at 5-10 deployments.
- **Worst case:** They acquire Hallo Kira. Then we differentiate on outcome depth (qualification, booking, dashboard) that RA-MICRO won't replicate in the acquisition.

**Monitoring:** RA-MICRO product announcements, Aktualisierungen page. If they announce "KI-Telefonassistenz" in a version update, it's time to accelerate.

### Risk 5: The 59%/68% Stat Doesn't Convert to Sales

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Probability** | MEDIUM (stat creates intellectual agreement, not urgency) |
| **Impact** | HIGH (sales cycle stalls, pipeline doesn't convert) |
| **Timeline** | Immediate (first 10 sales conversations) |
| **Kill shot?** | Not a kill shot, but a slow death. If managing partners consistently say "interesting" and do nothing, the model fails. |

**Mitigation:**
- **The call audit IS the mitigation.** The stat creates curiosity. The audit creates urgency.
- "59% hang up" is abstract. "47 people called your Kanzlei last week and got voicemail. 31 had real legal matters. 12 had Frist-relevant Arbeitsrecht cases. Here are their names and phone numbers." — THAT converts.
- Never sell without the audit. If they won't do a free 2-week audit, they're not our customer.
- Create a "Mandatsverlust-Rechner" (lost mandate calculator) for the landing page — plug in firm size, missed calls, average Erstberatung fee.

**Monitoring:** Track audit-to-pilot conversion rate. If <50%, the pain messaging needs rework.

### Risk 6: Solo Scaling Ceiling

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Probability** | CERTAIN (this will happen at 12-18 customers) |
| **Impact** | HIGH (quality collapses, churn increases, growth stalls) |
| **Timeline** | Month 5-8 |
| **Kill shot?** | Not if anticipated. Kill shot if ignored. |

**Mitigation:**
- **Plan the hire at customer 8, execute at customer 10-12.** Don't wait until you're drowning.
- **First hire:** German-speaking customer success person. €45-55K/year. Not a developer.
- **Template everything:** Practice area templates, onboarding checklists, monthly review scripts. The first hire should be able to onboard a new Arbeitsrecht firm in 4 hours using your template.
- **Automate:** Dashboard alerts replace daily manual checks. Automated monthly reports replace manual data pulls.
- **Funding:** At €7.5-9K MRR, you can afford the hire but it will eat into margin for 3-4 months. Save runway.

**Monitoring:** Track hours per customer per month. If >5 hours/customer at 10+ customers, you're in the danger zone.

### Risk 7: COGS Surprise (ElevenLabs Price Changes)

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Probability** | HIGH (LLM pass-through is stated as coming) |
| **Impact** | MEDIUM (30-50% COGS increase, compresses margins) |
| **Timeline** | 6-12 months |
| **Kill shot?** | Not alone. But combined with thin Basis tier margins, could make <€999/month pricing unprofitable. |

**Mitigation:**
- **Build pricing with headroom.** Current pricing (€499-1,999) has room for 30-50% COGS increase.
- **Consider own LLM.** ElevenLabs supports BYO LLM. Host a smaller model (Llama, Mistral) for routine qualification tasks. Use GPT-4/Claude for complex conversations only.
- **Diversify voice infrastructure.** Own platform (520K lines) means ElevenLabs is swappable. If COGS become unviable: evaluate Vapi, Retell, or open-source voice stack.
- **Pass through if needed.** If ElevenLabs costs rise 50%, raise Basis to €599 and Professional to €1,199. At these prices, we still undercut a Sekretärin.

**Monitoring:** Track ElevenLabs pricing announcements, developer forum discussions on cost changes. Calculate actual per-customer COGS monthly.
