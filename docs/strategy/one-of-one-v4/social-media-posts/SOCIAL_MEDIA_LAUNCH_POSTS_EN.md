## OGILVY PRINCIPLES APPLIED

| Principle | How It's Used |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"The headline does 80% of the work"** | Every post opens with a specific, arresting fact — not a generic claim |
| **"Facts are more persuasive than adjectives"** | 59%, 68%, $3,850/week, 34% to 2% — real numbers, not superlatives |
| **"The consumer is not a moron"** | No hype language, no "revolutionary" or "game-changing" — just the math |
| **"Tell the truth"** | Honest competitive comparison (Post 4), real customer data (Post 5) |
| **"Make the product the hero"** | Posts 1 and 3 let each agent's function speak for itself |
| **"Long copy sells — if it's interesting"** | Posts 1 and 5 are long because the story earns the length |
| **"First-person story"** | Post 5 uses founder narrative with a real customer case |
| **"Specificity"** | Employment law deadlines, $250-500 consultation, 12:35 on a Tuesday |
| **"Promise a benefit"** | "Free 14-day call audit" — low risk, high information value |
| **"Reason why"** | Post 4 gives four options and lets the reader decide |
