# Seven Layers — Visual Concepts for Social Media Posts

> Ogilvy on visuals: "If you can make your picture do the work, don't waste money on words."
> Every image should either prove the claim or provoke curiosity. Never decorate.

---

## Brand Constraints

- **Primary palette:** Black background, orange accent (#E8520A), white text
- **Typography:** Clean sans-serif, generous tracking (matches SEVENLAYERS wordmark)
- **Tone:** Professional, not corporate. Data-forward. No stock photo energy.

## POST 2 — The Problem Post

### Option A: The Calculator Visual
A clean, minimal graphic: a calculator/receipt design on black background. Line items in white:

```
47 incoming calls
-29 answered
= 18 missed
x 59% hang-up rate
x €350 avg. value
= €3,850 lost/week
```

Orange accent on the final total. No decorations. The math is the visual.

### Option B: The Missed Call Screen
A realistic phone screen showing a notification stack: "Missed Call (3)", "Missed Call", "Missed Call (2)" — timestamps showing lunch hour, evening, weekend. The phone sits on a desk next to a coffee cup. Shot from above, dark and moody.

### Option C: No Image
Just the text post. On LinkedIn, text-only posts often outperform image posts for engagement because the algorithm favors dwell time on text. The writing is strong enough to carry this solo. Use the image budget on Post 1 and Post 3.

## POST 3 — Meet the Team Carousel

### Option A: Existing Agent Cards (Current Assets)
The dark-themed agent cards with avatars, role labels, and orange CTAs. These are polished and product-authentic. Recommended if you want to show the real UI.

### Option B: "Baseball Card" Agent Profiles
Each slide designed like a trading card / player card:

- Agent avatar (large, top half)
- Name + role title in orange
- 3 key stats in a stat block (like a sports card):
  - Clara: "Answer rate: 100% | Avg. response: <3s | Languages: DE, EN"
  - Jonas: "Qualification accuracy: 95% | Routing time: <60s | Channels: 4"
  - Maren: "Scheduling conflicts: 0 | Fill rate: 98% | Integrations: 12"
- One-line description at bottom
- Black background, orange/agent-color accents

**Why this works:** Gamifies the agents. Makes people swipe to collect them all. Stat blocks feel credible and specific.

## GENERAL VISUAL GUIDELINES

### Do:
- Use the real product UI when showing features (authenticity > polish)
- Use large, bold numbers for stat posts (readable at phone thumbnail size)
- Keep text on images minimal — LinkedIn crops and compresses
- Use black backgrounds consistently (matches brand, stands out in feeds)
- Make agent avatars recognizable and recurring across posts (brand consistency)

### Don't:
- Use stock photos of smiling people in offices (kills credibility)
- Put long paragraphs on images (that's what the post text is for)
- Use blue/corporate color palettes (blend into every other B2B post)
- Add too many design elements (the data IS the design)
- Use "AI brain" or "robot" imagery (positions you as tech, not outcome)

### Tools for Creating These:
- **Figma** — For polished carousel slides and data graphics
- **Canva** — For quick iterations on stat cards and quote graphics
- **Screenshots** — Real product screenshots carry more trust than designed mockups
- **iPhone mockups** — For phone-screen visuals (missed call notification, demo call)
